# Refining Unused Access with IAM Access Analyzer

## Session Overview

This AWS re:Inforce 2024 session addresses one of the most persistent challenges in cloud security: achieving least privilege at scale. As organizations grow their AWS footprints, permissions tend to accumulate, and identifying which of those permissions are actually being used becomes increasingly difficult. The session demonstrates how AWS Identity and Access Management (IAM) Access Analyzer helps security teams and developers right-size permissions across an entire AWS organization, providing both visibility into unused access and actionable guidance to remediate it safely.

## Gaining Visibility into Unused Access

### Organization-Wide Analysis

The session emphasizes that least privilege is a continuous journey rather than a one-time project. IAM Access Analyzer addresses this by continuously monitoring permissions across an AWS organization and surfacing unused access patterns, including unused IAM roles, unused access keys, unused passwords, and unused service-level and action-level permissions within active roles. By centralizing these findings, security teams gain a unified view of their permission posture across many accounts without having to manually audit each one. This visibility allows organizations to prioritize their refinement efforts based on risk and impact, focusing first on identities and permissions that present the greatest exposure.

### Understanding Findings

Findings generated by IAM Access Analyzer give security and identity teams the context needed to make confident decisions. Rather than simply flagging that a permission exists, the analyzer reports on whether it has been exercised over a configurable tracking period. This distinction is critical because it shifts conversations from theoretical risk to evidence-based remediation, enabling teams to engage developers with concrete data when proposing permission reductions.

## Actionable Recommendations and Automated Remediation

### New Refinement Recommendations

A central focus of the session is the introduction of new IAM Access Analyzer recommendations that go beyond identifying unused access to providing prescriptive guidance on how to refine it. These recommendations supply developers and administrators with the specific changes needed to remove unused permissions while preserving the access required for legitimate workloads. By translating raw findings into ready-to-apply policy adjustments, the feature significantly lowers the friction of remediation and shortens the time between detection and resolution.

### Building Automated Workflows

The session also covers practical tips and tricks for operationalizing unused access management at scale. Rather than handling each finding manually, teams are encouraged to build automated workflows that route findings to the appropriate account owners, integrate with ticketing or notification systems, and apply remediation safely through established change-management processes. Automation transforms permission refinement from a periodic cleanup exercise into a continuous, low-overhead practice embedded in day-to-day operations.

## Outcomes and Takeaways

The overarching outcome the session promotes is the ability to refine permissions confidently and quickly, without inadvertently breaking workloads. By combining organization-wide visibility, evidence-based findings, prescriptive recommendations, and automation, IAM Access Analyzer enables organizations to make measurable progress toward least privilege. The key takeaway is that security teams no longer need to choose between developer velocity and tight permissions; with the right tooling and workflows, they can deliver both, steadily reducing the blast radius of compromised credentials while maintaining the agility that AWS customers expect.
