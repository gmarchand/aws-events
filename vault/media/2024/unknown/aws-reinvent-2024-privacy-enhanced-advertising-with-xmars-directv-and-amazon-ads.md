# Privacy-Enhanced Advertising with Xmars, DIRECTV, and Amazon Ads

## Overview of the Session

This AWS re:Invent 2024 session (ADM304) brings together Amazon Ads, Xmars, and DIRECTV to explore how modern advertising ecosystems can deliver effective, targeted campaigns while preserving consumer privacy. The discussion centers on Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC) and Amazon Publisher Cloud (APC), both built on AWS Clean Rooms, and how these technologies enable advertisers and publishers to collaborate on first-party data without exposing sensitive information. The session demonstrates how privacy-enhanced data collaboration is reshaping campaign planning, activation, and measurement through Amazon DSP.

## Privacy-Enhanced Data Collaboration in Modern Advertising

### The Shift Toward Clean Room Technology

The advertising industry is undergoing a fundamental transformation as third-party cookies decline and privacy regulations expand. In response, Amazon Ads has invested in clean room infrastructure that allows advertisers and publishers to bring their first-party data into a secure, governed environment. AWS Clean Rooms underpins both AMC and APC, ensuring that participating organizations can derive insights and activate audiences collaboratively without sharing raw user-level data. This approach preserves consumer privacy while enabling more relevant and effective advertising experiences.

### Amazon Marketing Cloud and Amazon Publisher Cloud

Amazon Marketing Cloud serves as the analytical hub for advertisers, allowing them to combine their own signals with Amazon Ads data to plan campaigns, build custom audiences, and measure performance with greater precision. Amazon Publisher Cloud extends similar capabilities to publishers, who can model and package their inventory using their first-party audience signals enriched with Amazon insights. Together, these platforms create a connected ecosystem where supply and demand sides collaborate through privacy-safe queries, with audience activation flowing seamlessly into Amazon DSP.

### AWS Clean Rooms ML for Audience Modeling

A central capability highlighted in the session is AWS Clean Rooms ML, which enables organizations to build lookalike audiences and similar models on collaborator data without exposing the underlying records. This unlocks new pathways for advertisers to expand reach beyond their known customer base while publishers retain control over their audience data. Reference architectures shared during the session illustrate how data ingestion, modeling, and activation pipelines connect to deliver these outcomes at scale.

## Customer Perspectives from Xmars and DIRECTV

### Xmars: Scaling Advertiser Outcomes

Xmars shares how it leverages AMC to help its advertiser clients plan and execute more sophisticated campaigns on Amazon DSP. By integrating client first-party data through clean rooms, Xmars produces richer audience definitions, refines targeting strategies, and improves measurement fidelity. The result is more efficient media spend and demonstrable performance gains for brands operating across Amazon's advertising surfaces.

### DIRECTV: Activating Publisher Inventory

DIRECTV illustrates the publisher side of the equation, using Amazon Publisher Cloud to package its premium streaming and television inventory with enriched audience signals. This allows DIRECTV to offer advertisers more precise reach across its viewer base while respecting subscriber privacy. The collaboration improves yield for DIRECTV and provides advertisers with high-quality, addressable inventory that aligns with their campaign objectives.

## Key Takeaways and Outcomes

The session establishes that privacy-enhanced advertising is no longer an aspiration but an operational reality, supported by mature tools from AWS and Amazon Ads. Advertisers gain deeper planning and measurement capabilities, publishers unlock new monetization pathways, and consumers benefit from more relevant ads delivered through privacy-respecting infrastructure. Attendees leave with reference architectures for implementing AMC, APC, and AWS Clean Rooms ML, along with concrete examples from Xmars and DIRECTV showing how these technologies translate into measurable business value. The overarching message is that data collaboration, when grounded in clean room technology, represents the next generation of impactful and responsible advertising.
