# AWS Unplugged: Reimagining Remote Music Creation with Universal Music Group

## Transforming the Traditional Studio Model

The music industry has long operated under the assumption that creative collaboration requires physical proximity. Artists, producers, and songwriters traditionally gather in the same studio to capture the spontaneous energy that defines great recordings. This session, presented at AWS re:Invent 2024, explores how Universal Music Group's Republic Records Studios is fundamentally challenging this paradigm through a partnership with AWS and Open Sesame, demonstrating that the future of music creation no longer depends on shared geography.

The collaboration centers on building a synchronous, bidirectional recording infrastructure that allows creative talent to share high-fidelity audio assets and creative ideas securely across distances. By leveraging AWS infrastructure, Republic Records aims to deliver this capability in a cost-effective and operationally efficient way, removing the friction that has historically slowed remote sessions or compromised their quality.

## The Technical Foundation for Real-Time Collaboration

### Synchronous Bidirectional Recording over AWS

At the heart of the solution is a system designed to handle the demanding requirements of professional music production, where latency, fidelity, and security all matter equally. The architecture supports two-way streaming of session assets so that an artist in one city can perform alongside producers and engineers in another as if they were sharing the same control room. AWS provides the global backbone needed to maintain low-latency synchronization, while the partnership with Open Sesame contributes the specialized workflows that integrate with existing studio environments.

This approach addresses several long-standing pain points: the cost and logistics of flying talent to a single location, the security risks of moving unreleased master assets between facilities, and the operational overhead of coordinating disparate toolchains. By centralizing the workflow on AWS, Republic Records gains a repeatable, scalable model that can be extended across its roster of artists and global studio network.

### Live Demonstration of Remote Creative Workflow

The session culminates in a live performance in which a recording artist reimagines one of their favorite songs while collaborating in real time with producers stationed at the world-famous Republic Records studio in Los Angeles. This demonstration serves as proof that the technical architecture supports not just file transfer or asynchronous review, but the genuine creative back-and-forth that defines a productive studio session. The performance illustrates how feedback, takes, and adjustments can flow seamlessly between locations without the perceptual delays that would otherwise break an artist's flow.

## Implications for the Future of Music Production

### A More Accessible and Secure Creative Environment

The outcomes of this collaboration extend beyond a single artist or label. By proving that synchronous remote recording can meet the standards of major-label production, UMG and AWS open the door to a more accessible creative economy in which geography no longer limits who can work with whom. Emerging artists in remote regions, producers with scheduling constraints, and global songwriting teams all stand to benefit from infrastructure that treats distance as a solved problem. At the same time, the emphasis on secure asset sharing protects the intellectual property that is the lifeblood of the industry, ensuring that broader access does not come at the cost of control. Together, these advances signal a meaningful shift in how the world's largest music company envisions the studio of the future—one built on cloud infrastructure rather than walls and wiring.
