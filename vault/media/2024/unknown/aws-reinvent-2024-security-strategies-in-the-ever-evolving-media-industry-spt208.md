# Security Strategies in the Ever-Evolving Media Industry

## Overview of the Panel Discussion

This AWS re:Invent 2024 session brought together cybersecurity leaders from Amazon, DraftKings, and the National Hockey League (NHL) to examine the unique security challenges facing today's media and entertainment industry. The panel explored how organizations operating across live sports, online gaming, and streaming entertainment must defend against an expanding threat landscape while maintaining the seamless user experiences their audiences demand. Through candid exchanges, the speakers illustrated how the convergence of live broadcasting, real-time wagering, and digital content distribution has created new attack surfaces that require coordinated, industry-wide responses.

## Core Security Challenges Across Media Verticals

### Protecting Live Events and Intellectual Property

A central theme of the discussion was the heightened risk profile of live sports and entertainment, where any disruption is immediately visible to millions of viewers and carries significant financial and reputational consequences. Panelists described how cyberattacks targeting live broadcasts, ticketing systems, and fan-facing platforms have grown more sophisticated, often timed to coincide with marquee events when attackers know defenders are most stretched. For entertainment companies, safeguarding intellectual property — from unreleased content to proprietary production workflows — remains paramount, requiring layered controls that protect assets throughout the content lifecycle without slowing creative output.

### Securing Real-Time Gaming and Wagering Platforms

DraftKings shared perspectives on the distinct demands of online gaming and sports betting, where transactions occur at high velocity and even momentary downtime erodes customer trust and revenue. The panel emphasized that fraud prevention, account takeover defense, and regulatory compliance must operate in concert, and that security teams need to architect controls that detect anomalies in real time without introducing friction that pushes legitimate users away.

### Cross-Industry Collaboration as a Defensive Multiplier

The speakers repeatedly returned to the value of collaboration across the media sector, arguing that adversaries often reuse tactics across targets and that shared intelligence accelerates collective defense. They highlighted the importance of building relationships with peers, cloud providers, and law enforcement before incidents occur, so that response playbooks can be activated quickly when minutes matter.

## The Impact of Generative AI on Security Programs

### Emerging Threats from Deepfakes and Synthetic Content

Generative AI emerged as a defining force shaping the panel's outlook. The speakers warned that deepfakes and synthetic media pose particularly acute risks for organizations whose brands depend on authentic athlete, celebrity, and broadcaster representation. Manipulated content can mislead fans, undermine sponsorship integrity, and create fraud opportunities, making content authenticity and provenance pressing concerns for media security leaders.

### Defensive Applications and Proactive Posture

At the same time, the panel framed generative AI as a powerful tool for defenders, capable of accelerating threat detection, automating routine analyst work, and improving the speed and quality of incident response. The leaders agreed that adopting AI-driven security capabilities thoughtfully — with appropriate guardrails — is essential to keep pace with adversaries who are already weaponizing the same technologies.

## Key Takeaways and Forward-Looking Outcomes

The session concluded with a shared call for proactive, intelligence-driven security strategies tailored to the realities of modern media. The panelists agreed that protecting live experiences, intellectual property, and user trust requires investment in resilient cloud architectures, deeper cross-industry partnerships, and a measured embrace of generative AI for both offense and defense. Their collective message was that security in media is no longer a back-office function but a strategic enabler of the fan experience, and that organizations willing to collaborate, innovate, and anticipate emerging threats will be best positioned to thrive in an industry defined by constant change.
