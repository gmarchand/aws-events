# How National Australia Bank Is Pioneering Innovation and DevOps

## Session Overview

This AWS re:Invent 2024 session, presented by Harness as an AWS Partner, examines how National Australia Bank (NAB), one of Australia's largest financial institutions serving more than nine million customers, transformed its software delivery practices to support a cloud-first strategy. Nick Durkin, Field CTO at Harness, leads the conversation alongside Andrew Brydon, Executive Chief Engineer at NAB, who shares the firsthand experience of modernizing engineering at scale within a heavily regulated industry. Together they explore how the combination of AWS infrastructure and the Harness software delivery platform enabled NAB to accelerate innovation while maintaining the security, reliability, and compliance demanded of a tier-one bank.

## NAB's Cloud-First Transformation Journey

### Strategic Drivers and Business Context

NAB embarked on its cloud-first journey in 2017, recognizing that the rapidly evolving financial landscape required a fundamentally different operating model. Customer expectations were shifting toward digital-first experiences, fintech competition was intensifying, and legacy infrastructure was constraining the bank's ability to deliver new features at the pace the market demanded. Leadership concluded that becoming a cloud-first leading financial company would require more than lifting workloads to AWS; it would require rethinking how software is built, tested, secured, and released across thousands of engineers. This realization framed the modernization effort as a developer experience and DevOps challenge as much as an infrastructure one.

### Modernizing Software Delivery With Harness on AWS

To translate the cloud-first ambition into measurable engineering outcomes, NAB adopted the Harness software delivery platform running on AWS. Andrew Brydon describes how standardizing pipelines, deployments, feature flags, and governance on a single platform allowed the bank to replace fragmented, hand-built CI/CD tooling with a consistent, policy-driven delivery experience. AWS provides the elastic foundation for compute, data, and managed services, while Harness orchestrates how code moves safely from commit to production. The combination shortened lead times, reduced change-failure rates, and gave platform teams a coherent way to embed security and compliance controls directly into the delivery process rather than bolting them on afterward.

### Empowering Engineers and Embedding Governance

A central theme of the discussion is the impact on engineers themselves. By abstracting repetitive pipeline work and providing self-service guardrails, NAB freed development teams to focus on customer-facing features rather than the mechanics of deployment. Automated approvals, verification steps, and progressive delivery techniques replaced manual change management, giving risk and audit functions stronger evidence with less friction. Brydon emphasizes that cultural change, executive sponsorship, and a willingness to standardize were as important as the tooling decisions in achieving adoption across a large engineering organization.

## Outcomes and Key Takeaways

The collaboration between NAB, Harness, and AWS demonstrates that even highly regulated enterprises can achieve modern DevOps velocity when platform investments are paired with disciplined engineering practices. NAB has moved meaningfully closer to its goal of becoming a cloud-first leading financial company, with faster, safer releases and a more productive engineering workforce. The session closes with practical guidance for other organizations considering a similar path: treat software delivery as a strategic capability, invest in a unified platform that engineers actually want to use, and align cloud adoption with measurable improvements in developer productivity and customer outcomes.
