# AWS for M&E: Cowgirls on the Moon

## Overview of the Project

AWS for Media & Entertainment unveiled *Cowgirls on the Moon*, a faux movie trailer designed to demonstrate the creative and technical possibilities of cloud-based content production. The project was produced in collaboration with The Modern Film Consortium, DNEG36, and Dimension, and serves as a showcase piece for AWS's broader vision of holistic, end-to-end filmmaking in the cloud. Rather than functioning as a traditional film release, the trailer acts as a proof of concept that highlights how modern productions can leverage distributed, cloud-native workflows to deliver cinematic results.

## Technical Approach and Cloud-Based Workflow

### Generative AI, Virtual Production, and VFX Integration

The trailer was crafted using a fully cloud-based pipeline that integrates generative AI–assisted virtual production with traditional live action, animation, and visual effects. AWS Deadline Cloud powered the VFX and rendering components, enabling scalable compute resources to handle the demanding workloads associated with high-end visual effects. By unifying these previously siloed disciplines into a single cloud workflow, the production team demonstrated how creative teams can collaborate more efficiently while reducing the friction typically associated with moving assets between on-premises systems and disparate vendors.

### Alignment with the MovieLabs 2030 Vision

The production was deliberately aligned with the principles of the MovieLabs 2030 Vision, an industry framework that outlines the future of cloud-based, software-defined filmmaking. This alignment underscores AWS's commitment to advancing industry-wide standards for secure, interoperable, and efficient content creation. The complete workflow was demonstrated at NAB Show 2024 within the AWS "studio in the cloud" experience at booth W1701, giving attendees a tangible look at how these principles translate into practice.

## Partner Ecosystem and Industry Collaboration

A robust partner ecosystem made the project possible, reflecting the collaborative nature of modern cloud production. Slalom contributed alongside a wide range of AWS Content Production Partners, including Leostream, Qumulo, Unreal Engine, Perforce, Cuebric, Wacom, Foundry, SideFX, AJA, Colorfront, Filmlight, Adobe, and Streambox. The breadth of these partnerships illustrates how AWS positions itself as a connective platform that brings together specialized tools across virtual production, asset management, rendering, color, editorial, and creative software, enabling productions of any scale to assemble best-in-class workflows entirely in the cloud.
