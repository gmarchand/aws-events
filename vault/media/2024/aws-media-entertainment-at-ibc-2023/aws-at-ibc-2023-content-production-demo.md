# AWS at IBC 2023: Content Production Demo

## Overview of the Cloud-Based Production Workflow

At IBC 2023, AWS showcased a comprehensive content production demonstration that illustrates how modern media organizations can transform their promo creation pipelines by leveraging cloud infrastructure. The demo presents a real-world workflow where creative teams collaborate across geographic boundaries, moving content fluidly from the camera through to the final delivered asset. By centralizing media assets in AWS, the demonstration highlights how production companies eliminate the bottlenecks traditionally associated with shipping physical drives, manual file transfers, and siloed editing environments.

The core message of the showcase centers on interconnectedness. Rather than treating acquisition, post-production, and finishing as discrete stages handled by separate facilities, AWS positions the cloud as a unifying fabric that allows every contributor — from the camera operator on set to the colorist in a finishing suite — to access the same source material in near real time. This approach accelerates turnaround for time-sensitive promotional content while preserving the creative quality and security expectations of broadcast and studio customers.

## From Set to Cloud to Edit Bay

### Camera Ingest and Acquisition

The workflow begins at the moment of capture, with content acquired directly at camera ingest and pushed rapidly from the set into AWS. This cloud-first acquisition step replaces traditional offload routines, where footage typically remains trapped on local media until it can be physically transported to a post house. By moving footage off the set immediately, production teams gain the ability to begin logging, reviewing, and editing material while shooting is still underway, dramatically compressing the schedule for promo delivery.

### Post-Production and Edit Bay Integration

Once the content lands in the cloud, it flows seamlessly into a post-production environment that mirrors the familiar edit-bay experience editors already know. The demonstration emphasizes cloud-to-ground video capabilities, meaning editors working on local workstations can interact with cloud-hosted assets as though they were sitting on a local SAN. This preserves established creative tools and muscle memory while extending the reach of those tools to remotely located collaborators. Editors, assistants, and producers can work on the same project concurrently, with changes and selects visible across the team without waiting for media to be duplicated or re-synced.

### Collaboration and Finishing

The final stage of the workflow demonstrates how finishing tasks — such as color, graphics, and quality control — fit naturally into the same connected environment. Because all contributors share a single source of truth in AWS, handoffs between departments become near-instantaneous, and version control is simplified. The result is a promo workflow in which creation, management, and finishing happen within one continuous, collaborative pipeline rather than across disconnected facilities.

## Key Outcomes and Takeaways

The demonstration reinforces several important outcomes for media and entertainment customers considering a move to cloud-based production. Speed improves significantly because content is available for editing the moment it is captured, supporting the rapid turnaround demanded by promotional and marketing teams. Collaboration expands beyond the walls of any single facility, enabling distributed creative teams to participate in remote and virtual production models. Cost and logistical overhead associated with physical media handling are reduced, while the familiar editorial experience preserves creative continuity.

Ultimately, the IBC 2023 content production demo positions AWS as the connective tissue of a modern, end-to-end media workflow — one that takes content from camera to finished promo without the friction of legacy infrastructure, and one that scales to support the increasingly distributed nature of today's production teams.
