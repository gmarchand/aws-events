# Driving Business Transition Through Migration to the AWS Cloud

## Overview of the Discussion

This session brings together influential broadcasters and content owners to share their experiences migrating from on-premises workloads to the AWS Cloud. The conversation centers on the practical lessons learned during these transitions, with participants reflecting candidly on what has succeeded, what has fallen short of expectations, and what guidance they would offer to organizations beginning their own cloud journeys. The overarching theme is how cloud migration drives meaningful business transformation in the media and entertainment industry, particularly in terms of flexibility, agility, and operational efficiency.

## Key Themes from the Migration Journeys

### Motivations and Business Drivers

Broadcasters and content owners pursued cloud migration primarily to escape the constraints of legacy on-premises infrastructure. The need for greater operational flexibility emerged as a central motivator, allowing media organizations to scale resources dynamically in response to fluctuating production demands, live event spikes, and evolving content distribution requirements. Agility in launching new services and adapting workflows to changing market conditions also played a decisive role, as did the pursuit of operational efficiency through reduced capital expenditure and the elimination of hardware refresh cycles. These drivers collectively shifted the conversation from technology modernization to genuine business transformation.

### Lessons from What Worked

Participants highlighted that successful migrations depended heavily on careful planning, strong executive sponsorship, and a willingness to rethink workflows rather than simply replicate on-premises processes in the cloud. Embracing managed services and cloud-native architectures unlocked the most significant gains in efficiency and innovation. Organizations that invested in upskilling their teams and fostering a cultural shift toward cloud-first thinking found that adoption accelerated and the business benefits materialized more quickly. Collaboration with AWS and trusted partners during the design and execution phases proved essential to navigating the complexity of media supply chains.

### Challenges and What Did Not Work

Not every aspect of the journey unfolded smoothly. Speakers acknowledged that lift-and-shift approaches often failed to deliver the expected returns, as moving legacy applications without re-architecting them carried over inefficiencies and limited the ability to leverage cloud-native capabilities. Underestimating the change management effort required across technical and operational teams created friction, and cost optimization proved to be an ongoing discipline rather than a one-time achievement. Integration with existing broadcast and production systems also presented hurdles that demanded iterative refinement.

### Advice for Organizations Starting Their Cloud Journey

The collective advice for newcomers emphasizes starting with a clear business outcome rather than a technology mandate. Leaders are encouraged to identify workloads that will benefit most from cloud-native transformation, build internal expertise early, and treat migration as an opportunity to redesign processes for the future rather than preserve the past. Establishing strong governance, financial accountability through FinOps practices, and a phased approach that delivers incremental value were all cited as critical success factors. Above all, the panel stressed that cloud migration is a continuous journey of optimization and innovation, not a single project with a defined end point.

## Outcomes and Takeaways

The session reinforces that migration to the AWS Cloud, when approached strategically, becomes a catalyst for broader business transition in the media and entertainment sector. The shared experiences demonstrate that flexibility, agility, and operational efficiency are achievable outcomes, but only when organizations commit to cultural change, workflow redesign, and ongoing optimization. For broadcasters and content owners weighing similar moves, the candid reflections from peers offer both encouragement and a realistic roadmap for navigating the complexities ahead.
