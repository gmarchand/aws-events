# Navigating Europe's Transition to IP

## Overview of the Industry Shift

European broadcasters are undergoing a fundamental transformation as they move from a predominantly linear distribution model to one centered on Internet Protocol (IP) delivery. This session brings together perspectives on how the regional broadcast landscape is adapting to changing consumer behaviors, where audiences increasingly expect on-demand, personalized, and multi-device experiences. The discussion frames this transition not simply as a technical upgrade, but as a strategic reorientation of how content is produced, distributed, and monetized across the continent.

## Opportunities and Challenges in the European Market

### Unlocking New Capabilities Through IP

The shift to IP opens significant opportunities for European broadcasters, including greater operational flexibility, the ability to scale workloads dynamically in the cloud, and faster innovation cycles for new services. By leveraging AWS media services and live cloud production capabilities, broadcasters can launch channels more quickly, experiment with new formats, and reach audiences across geographies without the constraints of traditional linear infrastructure. IP-based workflows also enable richer data insights, more targeted advertising, and improved personalization, all of which support new revenue models in an increasingly competitive streaming environment.

### Addressing Technical and Organizational Hurdles

Alongside these opportunities, broadcasters face notable challenges. Legacy infrastructure, deeply embedded linear playout systems, and longstanding operational processes make the transition complex and capital-intensive. Regulatory considerations across diverse European jurisdictions, concerns around latency and reliability for live events, and the need to retrain technical staff all add to the difficulty. Maintaining the quality and resilience that audiences expect from traditional broadcast — particularly for live news and sports — while simultaneously building out IP capabilities requires careful orchestration and a willingness to operate hybrid environments for an extended period.

## Strategies Being Deployed by Broadcasters

### Hybrid and Cloud-First Approaches

To navigate this transition, broadcasters are adopting pragmatic strategies that balance continuity with innovation. Many are pursuing hybrid architectures that allow them to run linear and IP workflows in parallel, gradually migrating functions such as encoding, playout, and distribution to the cloud. Others are taking a cloud-first approach for new channels and services, using managed AWS media services to reduce the operational burden and accelerate time to market. Investments in remote and cloud-based live production are enabling more agile coverage of events, while partnerships with technology providers help bridge skill gaps and reduce risk.

### Building for a Predominantly IP Future

Looking forward, the broadcasters represented in the discussion are positioning themselves for a future where IP delivery becomes the dominant model. This involves rethinking content supply chains end to end, embracing software-defined operations, and aligning organizational structures to support continuous, data-driven product development. The overarching outcome of the session is a clear message: while the path is complex, the combination of cloud infrastructure, evolving consumer demand, and competitive pressure makes the transition to IP both inevitable and strategically essential for European broadcasters seeking long-term relevance.
