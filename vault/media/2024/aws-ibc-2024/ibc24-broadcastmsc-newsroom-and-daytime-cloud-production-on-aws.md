# Cloud-Native Newsroom and Daytime Production on AWS

## Overview of the Demonstration

At IBC 2024, AWS presented an end-to-end demonstration of a cloud-native, story-centric newsroom designed to transform how broadcasters produce daytime and news programming. The showcase highlighted how news teams can collaborate seamlessly across geographies, extract greater value from their content and data assets, and respond elastically to shifting audience demands. By migrating traditionally on-premises broadcast workflows to the cloud, the demonstration illustrated a path toward reduced operational costs, accelerated content creation, and the integration of advanced AI and machine learning capabilities directly into editorial processes.

The central premise of the solution is that modern newsrooms must be both agile and intelligent. Stories, rather than individual assets or technical silos, sit at the heart of the workflow, allowing journalists, editors, and producers to work concurrently on the same narrative thread from ingest through distribution.

## Architecture and AWS Services

### Foundational AWS Infrastructure

The demonstration is built on a foundation of core AWS services that provide the performance, scalability, and reliability required for professional broadcast operations. Amazon FSx delivers high-performance file storage suitable for the demanding throughput requirements of video editing and post-production, while Amazon S3 serves as the durable object storage backbone for media assets across their full lifecycle. Amazon EC2 supplies the elastic compute capacity that powers production applications, enabling broadcasters to spin up resources on demand and scale them down when not needed, directly addressing the cost variability that has long challenged traditional broadcast facilities.

### Integrated Partner Solutions

Layered on top of the AWS foundation is an ecosystem of specialized broadcast partner solutions that together form a complete newsroom workflow. Mimir provides production asset management, organizing and surfacing media throughout the production process. Dina functions as the newsroom control system, orchestrating rundowns and editorial coordination. CuttingRoom delivers cloud-native editing capabilities optimized for collaborative, browser-based workflows, while Adobe brings high-performance editing for more demanding craft work. Vizrt contributes graphics generation, ensuring on-air visuals are produced and integrated within the same cloud environment. The integration of these tools demonstrates that broadcasters do not need to choose between best-of-breed applications and a unified cloud architecture.

## Workflow Capabilities and Business Outcomes

### Story-Centric Collaboration and AI Assistance

The workflow design enables multiple contributors to engage with a single story simultaneously, eliminating the handoff delays and version-control problems endemic to file-based production. AI and machine learning services are embedded throughout the process to assist with content creation tasks such as metadata generation, transcription, and asset discovery, allowing editorial staff to focus on storytelling rather than manual logistics. This story-centric model maximizes the long-term value of archived content by making it more discoverable and reusable across future productions.

### Operational Benefits for Broadcasters

By unifying live and file-based workflows in a single cloud environment, the solution allows news organizations to streamline operations that were previously fragmented across separate systems. Broadcasters gain the ability to scale capacity elastically in response to breaking news events or seasonal programming demands, paying only for the resources they actually consume. The combined effect is a meaningful reduction in infrastructure costs, faster time to air, and a more flexible foundation for delivering engaging daytime and newsroom content to audiences whose consumption preferences continue to evolve.
