# Intelligent Operations for Direct-to-Consumer Streaming: Monitoring and Observability

## Overview of the Operational Challenge

Direct-to-consumer (D2C) and over-the-top (OTT) streaming services face the demanding task of delivering high-quality video experiences to global audiences at scale. To achieve this, operators require timely monitoring data paired with comprehensive dashboards that surface actionable insights. Without end-to-end observability across the streaming workflow, support teams struggle to identify root causes of playback issues, optimize delivery costs, and maintain the quality of service that subscribers expect. This IBC24 demonstration from AWS addresses these operational challenges by showcasing how cloud-native visualization, content delivery, and generative AI services combine with specialized partner solutions to create an intelligent operations framework.

## Building Quality of Service Dashboards on AWS

### Visualization and Data Collection with Native AWS Services

The foundation of the operational architecture leverages Amazon QuickSight for visualization and Amazon CloudFront as the content delivery network. By capturing Common Media Client Data (CMCD) directly from CloudFront, streaming platforms gain rich, standardized telemetry about how clients are consuming media. This data feeds quality of service dashboards that provide a unified view of stream health from origin through last-mile delivery, allowing operators to monitor performance, detect anomalies, and respond proactively to degradations affecting viewers.

### Accelerating Troubleshooting with Generative AI

The demonstration extends observability with Amazon Q, which introduces generative AI capabilities into the operational workflow. Support engineers can pose natural language queries to investigate playback problems, dramatically reducing the time required to interpret telemetry, correlate signals across systems, and arrive at a diagnosis. By lowering the technical barrier to data exploration, Amazon Q empowers frontline support staff to resolve issues that would traditionally require deeper engineering involvement.

## Partner Solutions Enhancing the Streaming Workflow

### IMAX StreamAware and StreamSmart

The session also highlights IMAX's StreamAware, an automated quality control and quality assurance software designed to help video service providers deliver superior viewing experiences more efficiently and cost-effectively. Complementing this is StreamSmart, which targets the economics of content delivery by reducing bitrates between 15 and 25 percent without compromising perceived video quality. These savings translate directly into lower CDN delivery costs while preserving the visual fidelity that defines premium streaming services.

### MUX Data for Video Performance Insights

Rounding out the demonstration is MUX Data, which provides streaming operators with meaningful insights into the metrics that matter most for their video catalog. By integrating MUX Data alongside AWS-native tooling, operators gain a comprehensive analytics layer that complements infrastructure-level observability with viewer-centric performance intelligence.

## Outcomes and Operational Impact

By combining Amazon CloudFront, QuickSight, and Amazon Q with partner solutions from IMAX and MUX, streaming providers can achieve end-to-end observability, accelerate incident resolution through natural language troubleshooting, automate quality assurance, and substantially reduce delivery costs. The integrated approach demonstrated at IBC24 enables D2C operators to scale globally while maintaining the operational efficiency and viewer experience required to compete in the streaming market.
