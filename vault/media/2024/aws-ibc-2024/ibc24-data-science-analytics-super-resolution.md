# Super Resolution for Archived Video Content

## Overview of the Demonstration

At IBC24, AWS showcased a Super Resolution demonstration designed to revitalize legacy video libraries through advanced artificial intelligence. The solution runs on Amazon SageMaker and harnesses two cutting-edge upscaler AI models, Real-ESRGAN and SwinIR, to enhance the resolution and overall visual quality of archived video content. By applying sophisticated machine learning techniques, the demonstration transforms pixelated, blurry, or low-resolution footage into crisp, high-definition content suitable for today's viewing standards.

## Technical Approach and Capabilities

### Leveraging SageMaker and Specialized AI Models

The architecture relies on Amazon SageMaker as the underlying platform for deploying and running the AI models, providing a scalable and managed environment for inference workloads. Real-ESRGAN and SwinIR work in tandem to upscale resolution and sharpen visual details, producing output that significantly exceeds the original footage in clarity and definition. This approach allows media organizations to process large volumes of legacy content efficiently while benefiting from the cloud-native flexibility that SageMaker offers.

## Business Value and Use Cases

### Unlocking Monetization and Audience Engagement

The demonstration targets media companies, broadcasters, and content owners seeking to extract renewed value from historical assets. By transforming archival footage into modern, high-quality content, organizations can repurpose and redistribute their libraries across contemporary platforms and devices, opening new monetization streams that were previously limited by outdated visual quality. Beyond commercial benefits, the enhanced visual fidelity strengthens viewer engagement and satisfaction, making historical and nostalgic material substantially more appealing to today's audiences and ensuring that valuable content continues to resonate well into the future.
