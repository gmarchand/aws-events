# Broadcast Operations, Monitoring, and Control on AWS

## Bringing Master Control Room Visibility to the Cloud

Broadcasters increasingly migrate channel playout and distribution workloads to AWS to gain the flexibility, cost efficiency, and elastic performance that cloud infrastructure provides. However, this transition has historically come with a trade-off: operators accustomed to the deep visibility and granular control of on-premises master control rooms often find cloud environments opaque by comparison. This IBC24 demonstration addresses that gap directly by presenting an end-to-end monitoring and observability solution that replicates the familiar MCR experience in the cloud, ensuring broadcasters do not have to compromise operational oversight to gain the benefits of AWS.

## An Integrated Observability Environment

### Architecture and Components

The demonstrated environment combines native AWS services, open-source software, and AWS Partner solutions to deliver a unified monitoring layer that spans the entire signal path. Rather than relying on a single tool, the architecture stitches together complementary technologies so that operators can observe video and audio quality in real time alongside the underlying compute, network, and application telemetry. This holistic approach mirrors the visibility broadcasters expect from traditional broadcast facilities, where every link in the chain is instrumented and reportable.

### Operator-Centric Workflow

A key design principle of the solution is accessibility for traditional broadcast operators who may not possess specialized cloud engineering skills. The interface allows these users to spin up playout channels and distribution pipelines on demand, abstracting away the complexity of provisioning AWS resources. Once channels are running, operators receive a comprehensive view of signal flow with real-time alerts at the video, audio, infrastructure, and application levels, enabling rapid identification and remediation of issues without requiring deep AWS expertise.

### Real-Time Monitoring and Alerting

The system provides continuous quality assessment of media streams while simultaneously surfacing performance metrics from the cloud infrastructure that supports them. By correlating application-level events with infrastructure health, the platform gives engineers the diagnostic depth needed to maintain broadcast-grade reliability. Alerts are tuned to the operational vocabulary of broadcast professionals, making the cloud environment feel like a natural extension of an existing MCR rather than a separate technical domain.

## Industry Recognition and Outcome

The significance of this work was validated when the demonstration received a NAB Show 2024 Product of the Year Award in the Monitoring and Measuring Tools category. This recognition underscores that the solution effectively closes the observability gap that has historically slowed broadcaster adoption of cloud-based playout and distribution. By enabling MCR-style environments on AWS without sacrificing automation or visibility, the demonstration provides broadcasters with a credible path to modernize their operations while retaining the control they require to deliver reliable, high-quality content to their audiences.
