# CBS Sports Digital: All In on the Cloud

## Introduction to a Cloud-First Broadcast Vision

CBS Sports Digital has taken a decisive step into the future of broadcasting by launching the Golazo Show entirely in the cloud. This fireside chat explores how the organization moved beyond traditional broadcast infrastructure to embrace a fully cloud-native production and distribution model. The conversation centers on the strategic motivations, technical execution, and operational outcomes of running a live sports show without relying on physical broadcast facilities.

## Building a Fully Cloud-Native Broadcast Workflow

### Contribution and Master Control in the Cloud

The Golazo Show represents a comprehensive reimagining of the broadcast chain. Contribution feeds, traditionally routed through fiber and satellite into on-premises master control rooms, are now ingested and managed directly in the cloud. This shift removes the dependency on physical routing equipment and allows CBS Sports Digital to flexibly source content from multiple locations without the constraints of fixed infrastructure. Master control operations, including signal monitoring, switching, and quality control, similarly run as cloud-based services, giving operators the ability to manage the broadcast from virtually anywhere.

### Playout and Distribution at Scale

Playout, the function that schedules and pushes content to air, has likewise been migrated to the cloud, enabling automated and software-defined control over what viewers see and when. Distribution to digital endpoints follows a cloud-native path as well, taking advantage of scalable streaming infrastructure to reach audiences across platforms. By unifying contribution, master control, playout, and distribution within a single cloud environment, CBS Sports Digital has eliminated many of the handoffs and integration points that traditionally introduce latency, cost, and operational complexity.

### Operational Benefits and Strategic Outcomes

The decision to go all in on the cloud has delivered measurable advantages. CBS Sports Digital can spin up production capacity on demand, scale resources to match the needs of specific events, and avoid the capital expenditure associated with building and maintaining traditional broadcast facilities. Remote collaboration becomes straightforward when every component of the workflow is accessible through cloud services, allowing talent, producers, and engineers to contribute from distributed locations. The approach also positions the organization to iterate quickly, adopting new tools and capabilities as the cloud ecosystem evolves rather than being locked into hardware refresh cycles.

## Conclusion

The Golazo Show stands as a proof point that live sports broadcasting can operate end to end in the cloud, from initial contribution through final distribution. CBS Sports Digital's experience demonstrates that committing fully to a cloud-based workflow unlocks agility, scalability, and cost efficiency while still meeting the demanding quality and reliability standards of live sports. This case offers a clear blueprint for other media organizations considering a similar transformation.
