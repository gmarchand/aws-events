# Real-Life Automation and Security Best Practices from the Field

## Overview of the Session

At AWS re:Invent 2023, Fortinet presented session COP228, focused on helping organizations operationalize and secure their cloud deployments on AWS. As enterprises continue to advance their application journeys into the cloud and adopt hybrid cloud approaches, Fortinet has observed recurring challenges around automation, governance, and security posture management. This session brought together a panel of Fortinet experts and customer practitioners to share lessons learned throughout 2023 and to translate those insights into practical recommendations that organizations can adopt to accelerate their own cloud transformations.

## Key Themes and Best Practices

### Operationalizing Cloud Security at Scale

A central theme of the discussion was the difficulty enterprises face when moving from initial cloud adoption to mature, large-scale operations. The panel emphasized that security must be embedded into deployment pipelines from the outset rather than retrofitted later. Fortinet's field experience shows that organizations succeed when they treat infrastructure as code, codify security policies alongside application templates, and use automation to enforce consistency across accounts, regions, and hybrid environments. By integrating Fortinet security controls with AWS-native services, customers reduce manual configuration drift and maintain a defensible security posture as their footprint expands.

### Automation as the Foundation for Hybrid Cloud

The panelists stressed that automation is the connective tissue between on-premises environments and AWS workloads. Customers shared how they use automated provisioning, policy synchronization, and event-driven response workflows to maintain unified visibility and control across hybrid architectures. The discussion highlighted that successful organizations standardize on repeatable patterns—templated network segmentation, automated firewall rule deployment, and centralized logging—so that scaling new workloads does not introduce new security gaps. This automation-first mindset also accelerates incident response by enabling faster detection, containment, and remediation.

### Customer Lessons and Practical Recommendations

Drawing on real-world deployments, the customer panelists described how they overcame common pitfalls such as fragmented tooling, inconsistent identity management, and limited cross-team collaboration. Their guidance centered on starting with clear governance frameworks, investing early in cloud security skills, and choosing partner solutions that integrate natively with AWS services to avoid operational silos. The panel encouraged attendees to prioritize observability, adopt shared responsibility models explicitly within their teams, and continuously revisit architectures as workloads evolve.

## Conclusion and Takeaways

The session closed with a clear message: cloud success depends as much on disciplined operational practices as on technology choices. By combining AWS's breadth of services with Fortinet's security automation capabilities and the lived experience of mature cloud customers, organizations can shorten their path to a secure, scalable, and well-governed cloud environment. Attendees were encouraged to apply these field-tested practices—automation-led deployment, integrated security, and strong governance—as they plan and execute their next phase of cloud adoption.
