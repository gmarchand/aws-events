# Bridging Research and Computing to Tackle the World's Grand Challenges

## Overview of the Session

This AWS re:Invent 2023 session (WPS207) brought together experts from leading academic research organizations to explore how cloud computing, high performance computing (HPC), and artificial intelligence and machine learning (AI/ML) are transforming the way researchers address some of the most pressing challenges facing humanity. The discussion centered on a core premise: when researchers are constrained by limited access to data or prohibitively expensive computing infrastructure, the cloud offers a transformative pathway forward, enabling scientists to concentrate on discovery rather than logistics.

## The Intersection of Research and Cloud Computing

### Tackling Grand Challenges Through Technology

The conversation emphasized that issues such as mitigating the impacts of climate change and ensuring global food security demand computational capabilities that often exceed what traditional on-premises research environments can provide. By leveraging AWS cloud services, universities and research institutions can scale resources elastically, accommodate massive datasets, and run complex simulations and models that would otherwise be impractical. The panelists highlighted how this democratization of compute power levels the playing field, allowing researchers across institutions of varying sizes and budgets to pursue ambitious scientific questions.

### High Performance Computing and AI/ML as Research Accelerators

A significant portion of the discussion focused on the role of HPC and AI/ML in accelerating research outcomes. Cloud-based HPC enables on-demand access to specialized hardware, including GPUs and high-memory instances, that researchers can spin up for specific workloads and shut down when no longer needed. Combined with AI/ML services, this approach allows scientists to extract insights from vast datasets, build predictive models, and iterate on hypotheses far more rapidly than traditional methods permit. The panelists shared how their institutions have adopted these capabilities to drive progress in domains ranging from environmental science to agricultural research.

### Empowering Researchers to Focus on Science

A recurring theme throughout the session was the importance of removing infrastructure burdens from researchers so they can dedicate their time to scientific inquiry. By partnering with AWS, the represented universities have built environments where data access, compute provisioning, and tool integration are streamlined. This shift not only improves productivity but also fosters collaboration across disciplines and institutions, since cloud-based platforms make it easier to share data, reproduce results, and engage in joint research initiatives.

## Key Takeaways and Outcomes

The session concluded with a clear message: the cloud has become an indispensable enabler of modern research, particularly for tackling grand challenges that require interdisciplinary collaboration and substantial computational resources. The featured academic research organizations demonstrated that strategic adoption of cloud technologies, HPC, and AI/ML allows their researchers to pursue more ambitious questions, deliver results faster, and contribute meaningfully to solving global problems. For institutions evaluating their own research computing strategies, the panel underscored the value of investing in cloud-based infrastructure and partnerships as a means of empowering scientists and amplifying their impact on the world.
