# Moving On-Premises Broadcast Workloads to the Cloud

## Overview of the Cloud Shift in Live Broadcasting

Global broadcasters are increasingly placing cloud migration at the top of their strategic agendas, especially for operations that include a substantial live broadcasting component. What was once considered a workload too sensitive and latency-critical for the cloud has now been firmly validated through marquee events such as the Olympics, where cloud technologies played a central role in delivering live content at scale. This shift signals a broader industry consensus: the cloud is not only viable for live events, sports, and news, but is rapidly becoming the preferred infrastructure for modern broadcast operations. The session, presented by AWS Partner Amagi, framed this transition as both a technological and a business transformation, emphasizing how broadcasters can rethink the economics and agility of their live operations by leveraging AWS.

## Amagi Dynamic on AWS and the New Broadcast Economics

### Eliminating Upfront Hardware and Embracing Pay-as-You-Go Agility

A central theme of the talk is how Amagi Dynamic, running on AWS, fundamentally changes the cost structure of live broadcasting. Traditional broadcast operations require significant upfront capital investment in dedicated hardware, much of which sits idle between events. Amagi's solution removes this barrier by enabling broadcasters to spin up live workflows on demand and pay only for the resources they consume. This consumption-based model gives broadcasters the flexibility to pursue short-term events, seasonal programming, and pop-up channels without locking themselves into long-term infrastructure commitments. The result is an operational model where agility, rather than capacity planning, drives the broadcast calendar.

### Live Pop-Ups as a Low-Risk Path to Cloud Migration

Amagi positions live pop-up channels as a practical entry point for broadcasters who want to test-drive cloud migration before committing to a full transition. By using these temporary, event-driven channels, broadcasters can validate cloud workflows, evaluate performance, and build internal confidence in AWS-based operations. This incremental approach lowers the perceived risk of migration and allows organizations to develop the operational muscle needed to eventually move core workloads to the cloud.

### Monetization, Audience Reach, and Enhancing FAST Channels

Beyond cost and agility, the cloud unlocks new revenue and distribution opportunities. Amagi highlighted how broadcasters can maximize content monetization by repurposing existing libraries into new live experiences and by reaching audiences across more platforms and geographies than traditional infrastructure would allow. A particularly important growth area is the integration of live content into Free Ad-Supported Streaming Television (FAST) channels. By enhancing FAST offerings with live programming—such as breaking news, sports, and event coverage—broadcasters can increase viewer engagement, extend session times, and command stronger advertising value, all while operating on a flexible cloud backbone.

## Key Takeaways

The session's core message is that AWS, combined with partner solutions like Amagi Dynamic, has matured to the point where live broadcast workloads can be migrated to the cloud confidently and economically. Broadcasters who embrace this shift gain the ability to launch events without hardware constraints, experiment through pop-up channels, monetize content across more surfaces, and bring live energy to the rapidly growing FAST ecosystem. The decision facing broadcasters is no longer whether the cloud can support live operations, but how quickly they can adopt it to stay competitive in an increasingly dynamic media landscape.
