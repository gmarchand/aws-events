# Riot Games' Cloud Transformation Journey on AWS

## From Single-Title Studio to Global Cloud-First Gaming Company

Riot Games, the studio behind iconic titles such as League of Legends and VALORANT, has undergone a sweeping infrastructure transformation to support hundreds of millions of players worldwide. Brent Rich, head of global infrastructure and operations at Riot Games, recounts how the company evolved from a single-game studio reliant on regional, on-premises data centers into a cloud-first gaming company operating multiple major titles on a unified global architecture. The driving motivation behind this shift was the need to deliver consistently low-latency, highly competitive experiences to a globally distributed player base — a non-negotiable requirement for fast-paced multiplayer games where every millisecond influences the player experience.

## Building a Global, Low-Latency Architecture with AWS

### Leveraging Regions, Local Zones, and Outposts for VALORANT's Launch

The launch of VALORANT served as a pivotal proving ground for Riot's cloud strategy. Rather than provisioning physical infrastructure in every market, Riot combined AWS Regions, AWS Local Zones, and AWS Outposts to extend cloud capacity precisely where players needed it. This hybrid approach allowed the company to meet aggressive latency targets in regions where AWS Regions alone could not satisfy competitive gameplay requirements, while still benefiting from the consistency, automation, and scalability of a unified cloud platform. The result was a successful global rollout to millions of players, validating the viability of running tactical, latency-sensitive shooters on AWS infrastructure.

### Reinventing Esports Broadcasting in Days

When global circumstances disrupted traditional in-person esports production, Riot turned to AWS to rebuild its broadcasting pipeline as a remote-first solution. Rich explains that what would normally have been a multi-month engineering effort was compressed into a matter of days, enabling commentators, producers, and production crews to deliver professional-grade tournament broadcasts from distributed locations. This rapid pivot demonstrated the agility the cloud unlocked for Riot, allowing the company to preserve the continuity of its competitive scene without compromising broadcast quality.

### Migrating League of Legends to a Unified Cloud Architecture

Perhaps the most significant outcome Rich discusses is the migration of League of Legends — Riot's flagship title and one of the longest-running live-service games in the industry — from legacy on-premises data centers onto AWS. Consolidating League of Legends alongside VALORANT and other titles on a single cloud architecture provides Riot with operational consistency, simpler global capacity management, and a foundation for faster innovation across its portfolio. The migration represents the culmination of Riot's cloud-first strategy, replacing fragmented regional infrastructure with a coherent platform purpose-built for live, competitive gaming at scale.

## Outcomes and Strategic Direction

The overarching outcome of Riot's partnership with AWS is a globally unified, latency-optimized infrastructure that supports the company's entire game portfolio while accelerating its ability to launch new titles, host esports events, and respond to changing operational demands. By standardizing on AWS, Riot has reduced the friction of expanding into new regions, improved its responsiveness during major live events, and positioned itself to continue innovating in competitive online gaming. Rich frames the transformation not as a one-time migration but as an ongoing strategic alignment between Riot's player-first mission and the elasticity, reach, and reliability of the AWS cloud.
