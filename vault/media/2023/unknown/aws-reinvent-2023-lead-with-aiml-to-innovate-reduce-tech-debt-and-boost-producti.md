# Leading with AI/ML to Innovate, Reduce Tech Debt, and Boost Productivity

## Overview of the Session

This AWS re:Invent 2023 session (SEG205) explores how artificial intelligence and machine learning can help businesses tackle one of the most persistent obstacles to innovation: technical debt. Technical debt slows organizations down, surfaces as unexpected production issues, complicates scaling efforts, and forces difficult prioritization decisions between maintaining legacy systems and building new capabilities. The session presents AI as a strategic lever to address these challenges while simultaneously increasing developer productivity and delivering richer customer experiences. Shutterstock joins AWS to share a real-world account of how they applied AI to modernize their platform, reduce accumulated debt, and accelerate the delivery of new features that delight their customers.

## Key Themes and Discussion Points

### Reframing Technical Debt Through an AI Lens

The discussion centers on the idea that technical debt is not purely an engineering concern but a business constraint that directly impacts speed to market, customer satisfaction, and competitive positioning. By introducing AI capabilities into the modernization conversation, leaders can shift from reactive maintenance toward proactive transformation. The session emphasizes the importance of gaining better visibility into where debt accumulates, understanding its true cost, and recognizing that legacy design limitations often prevent teams from adopting modern architectures and patterns. AI tools provide new ways to analyze codebases, identify bottlenecks, and automate remediation work that previously consumed significant engineering capacity.

### Shutterstock's Journey with AI-Driven Modernization

Shutterstock shares its experience using AI and machine learning to reduce technical debt while simultaneously boosting engineering productivity. The company describes how AI-driven approaches enabled them to modernize core systems, automate repetitive engineering tasks, and free their teams to focus on innovation. The result has been a stronger ability to ship new features that enhance the customer experience, demonstrating that addressing tech debt and innovating are not competing priorities when AI is part of the strategy. Their story illustrates how a thoughtful application of AI can convert what was once a maintenance burden into a competitive advantage.

### Build versus Buy and Total Cost of Ownership

A significant portion of the session is devoted to the strategic evaluation of build versus buy decisions when adopting AI capabilities. The speakers walk through a comprehensive framework for assessing total cost of ownership that goes beyond initial implementation expenses to include ongoing maintenance, scaling considerations, talent requirements, and opportunity costs. This framework helps leaders make informed decisions about which AI capabilities to build internally for differentiation and which to acquire through managed services or third-party solutions to accelerate time to value.

## Outcomes and Takeaways

The session leaves attendees with a clearer understanding of how to approach technical debt strategically rather than tactically, using AI as both a productivity enabler and a modernization catalyst. Leaders are encouraged to evaluate their portfolios with a dual lens that considers both business impact and engineering health, to apply rigorous build-versus-buy analysis grounded in total cost of ownership, and to learn from Shutterstock's example of pairing AI adoption with deliberate modernization efforts. The overarching message is that organizations willing to lead with AI/ML can simultaneously reduce tech debt, increase developer productivity, and deliver more compelling customer experiences, turning a traditional cost center into a source of innovation.
