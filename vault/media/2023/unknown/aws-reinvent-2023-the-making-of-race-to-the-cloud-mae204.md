# The Making of "Race to the Cloud": Virtual Production on AWS

## Overview of the Session

At AWS re:Invent 2023, Brandon Lindauer, Senior Solutions Architect for AWS Media and Entertainment, and Katrina King, AWS Media and Entertainment Content Production Strategy Lead, delivered a lightning talk detailing the creation of "Race to the Cloud," a short film produced for NAB Show 2023. The presentation showcased how virtual production techniques—traditionally tethered to on-premises infrastructure—can now be fully accelerated and executed in the cloud using AWS services. The film served as a practical demonstration of an end-to-end motion picture pipeline running entirely on AWS, completed in just a few weeks.

## Reimagining Virtual Production in the Cloud

### From On-Premises Constraints to Cloud Flexibility

Virtual production has historically required substantial on-premises hardware investments, including LED volumes, render farms, and locally networked workstations. The session emphasized that AWS now removes these constraints, enabling filmmakers to spin up production environments on demand, scale rendering and visual effects workloads elastically, and collaborate globally without shipping physical assets between facilities. This shift makes virtual production a more flexible and affordable option for film, episodic, and commercial content creators of all sizes.

### Building the Production Pipeline on AWS

The team walked through how each phase of "Race to the Cloud" leveraged AWS services to streamline production. Pre-production tasks such as virtual scouting, asset creation, and pre-visualization were performed using cloud-based virtual workstations, allowing distributed artists to access high-performance environments from anywhere. During principal photography, real-time rendering for the LED volume was supported by cloud-accelerated workflows, while dailies, editorial, and visual effects iterations moved through cloud storage and processing services. Post-production color, sound, and final delivery were similarly handled within the AWS environment, demonstrating a continuous, cloud-native pipeline from script to screen.

### Speed, Collaboration, and Creative Outcomes

A central theme was the dramatic compression of the production timeline. By eliminating hardware procurement delays and enabling parallel, remote collaboration among creative teams, the filmmakers completed the project in a matter of weeks rather than months. The presenters highlighted how this approach not only reduced cost and logistical overhead but also expanded creative possibilities, giving directors and artists faster feedback loops and the ability to iterate on shots that would otherwise be locked in by physical production constraints.

## Key Takeaways for Media and Entertainment Teams

The session concluded by framing "Race to the Cloud" as a blueprint that media and entertainment customers can adapt to their own productions. Lindauer and King encouraged attendees to consider how cloud-based virtual production can democratize access to high-end filmmaking techniques, support globally distributed creative teams, and deliver high-quality content at the speed modern audiences demand. The film stands as proof that AWS can power the entire motion picture production lifecycle, signaling a broader shift in how the industry approaches content creation.
