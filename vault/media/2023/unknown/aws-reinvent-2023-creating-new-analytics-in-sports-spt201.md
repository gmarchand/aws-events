# Creating New Analytics in Sports: How the NHL and AWS Transform the Game

## The Evolution from Box Scores to Tracking Data

Sports analytics has undergone a profound transformation, moving beyond traditional box scores and basic statistics into the era of granular tracking data. This shift represents a fundamental change in how raw data is captured, processed, and interpreted across professional sports. Modern tracking technology now records the precise movements of players, pucks, balls, and equipment many times per second, generating massive datasets that were previously impossible to obtain. This rich foundation of positional and movement data has opened entirely new possibilities for understanding the nuances of athletic performance, tactical decision-making, and game flow that traditional statistics could never reveal.

The National Hockey League, in partnership with AWS, has been at the forefront of this transformation. By embedding sensors in pucks and players' equipment, the NHL captures detailed spatiotemporal data throughout every game. This raw stream of information serves as the starting point for a sophisticated analytics pipeline that turns coordinates and timestamps into meaningful insights for coaches, broadcasters, and fans alike.

## Applying AI and Machine Learning to Surface New Insights

Once tracking data is captured, the real challenge becomes converting it into actionable intelligence. AWS and the NHL apply artificial intelligence and machine learning models to interpret these vast datasets, identifying patterns and generating advanced metrics that quantify aspects of the game previously left to subjective observation. Machine learning models can recognize specific play types, evaluate the quality of scoring opportunities, measure skating speed and efficiency, and assess defensive positioning with mathematical precision. The cloud infrastructure provided by AWS enables the scalability and computational power required to process this data in near real-time, ensuring that insights can be delivered while a game is still in progress.

### From Conception to On-Air Presentation

Creating a new analytic is a deliberate, multi-stage process that begins with a hypothesis or question about the sport. Data scientists, hockey experts, and engineers collaborate to define what the metric should measure and why it matters to the game. They then develop and validate the underlying models against historical data, refining the methodology until the analytic produces reliable and meaningful results. Once validated, the metric must be packaged for consumption — whether by team analysts, league operations, or broadcast partners.

The final stage involves bringing the analytic to the screen during live broadcasts, which requires tight integration between the data pipeline and broadcast production workflows. Visualizations must be intuitive enough for fans to grasp instantly while remaining accurate to the underlying statistical work. This end-to-end journey, from raw sensor input to on-air graphic, exemplifies how cloud computing and machine learning are reshaping the way sports are coached, trained for, and consumed by audiences around the world.

## Impact on Coaching, Training, and Fan Engagement

The new generation of analytics influences every level of the sport. Coaches gain objective tools to evaluate player performance and tactical execution, training staffs use data to optimize conditioning and recovery, and broadcasters can tell richer stories that deepen fan engagement. The collaboration between the NHL and AWS demonstrates a repeatable model for how leagues can leverage cloud-based AI and ML capabilities to continuously innovate, ensuring that the analytics powering modern sports keep pace with the speed and complexity of the games themselves.
