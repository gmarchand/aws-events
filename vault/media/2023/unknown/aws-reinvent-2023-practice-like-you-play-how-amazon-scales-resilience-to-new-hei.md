# Practice Like You Play: How Amazon Scales Resilience to New Heights

## Building a Culture of Continuous Resilience at Amazon Prime Video

Amazon Prime Video has emerged as a pioneer in continuous resilience and chaos engineering at massive scale, supporting a global streaming platform that millions of customers depend on daily. The session explores how the Prime Video team developed a proven, repeatable process guided by the motto "Think global, act local," which empowers individual teams to take ownership of their services while aligning with organization-wide resilience goals. This philosophy has enabled Prime Video to build a sustainable culture where high availability and service reliability are not afterthoughts, but foundational principles embedded in every stage of the development lifecycle.

The presentation emphasizes that scaling resilience across a complex, distributed organization requires more than tooling—it demands a cultural shift. By making operational excellence, service resilience, observability, and reporting the top priorities, Prime Video has been able to meet exceptionally high customer demands during peak events such as live sports streaming and major content launches. The team's approach treats resilience as a continuous practice rather than a one-time achievement, ensuring that systems remain robust as they evolve.

## Practicing Failure Through GameDays and Chaos Engineering

### Dress Rehearsals and Proactive Failure Injection

A central theme of the session is the importance of practicing failure scenarios before they occur in production. Prime Video runs structured GameDays and dress rehearsals where teams deliberately inject failures into their systems to validate assumptions, test recovery procedures, and uncover hidden weaknesses. These exercises mirror real-world conditions as closely as possible, allowing engineers to build muscle memory for incident response. Chaos engineering practices extend this discipline by continuously and systematically introducing controlled disruptions, ensuring that resilience claims are validated through evidence rather than assumed through design documents.

### Driving Accountability with the Operational Readiness Score

To make resilience measurable and actionable across the organization, Prime Video developed an Operational Readiness Score that quantifies the maturity of services across multiple resilience domains. This score serves as both a diagnostic tool and a forcing function, giving teams a clear view of where they stand and what improvements are needed. By translating abstract resilience concepts into concrete metrics, the readiness score drives consistent investment in reliability across diverse engineering teams and ultimately translates into measurable increases in customer satisfaction and trust.

## Outcomes and Lessons for the Broader Community

The session concludes by demonstrating how the combination of cultural commitment, structured failure practice, and quantitative measurement has allowed Prime Video to deliver the dependable streaming experience customers expect. The key takeaway is that resilience at scale is achieved not through heroic efforts during incidents, but through deliberate, repeated practice during calm periods—truly practicing like you play. Organizations seeking to replicate this success are encouraged to invest equally in the technical practices of chaos engineering and observability and in the organizational practices of shared ownership, transparent scoring, and continuous learning.
