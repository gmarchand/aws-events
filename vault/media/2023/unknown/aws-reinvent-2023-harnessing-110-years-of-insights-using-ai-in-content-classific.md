# Harnessing 110 Years of Insights: Using AI in Content Classification

## Session Overview

This AWS re:Invent 2023 session presents a collaborative effort between the British Board of Film Classification (BBFC) and AWS Professional Services to modernize content classification through artificial intelligence. Founded in 1912, the BBFC has spent over 110 years assigning age ratings and compliance guidance to films and other media in the United Kingdom. The organization's mission centers on helping audiences—particularly families—make safe and informed viewing choices, and its longevity has produced an extraordinarily rich archive of classification decisions, compliance notes, and contextual judgments. The session explores how this institutional knowledge can be transformed into a scalable, multimodal AI solution capable of meeting the demands of an exploding volume of digital content.

## The Challenge and the Approach

### Scaling a Century of Human Expertise

The central challenge driving the project is one of scale. Traditional human-led classification, while nuanced and authoritative, cannot keep pace with the volume of streaming, online, and short-form content that modern audiences consume. The BBFC needed a way to extend the reach of its expert classifiers without compromising the careful, context-aware judgments that have defined its work for over a century. Rather than replacing human reviewers, the project aims to augment them, applying AI to triage, pre-classify, and surface the issues that matter most for compliance decisions. The team treated the BBFC's archive of historical classification data as a strategic asset, using it to ground machine learning models in decades of consistent, well-documented decisions.

### Building a Multimodal AI Solution

Working with AWS Professional Services, the BBFC developed a multimodal solution that analyzes content across several dimensions simultaneously, including visual imagery, spoken dialogue, audio cues, and on-screen text. By combining these signals, the system can detect classification-relevant elements such as violence, language, and other thematic content with greater accuracy than any single modality would allow. The architecture leverages AWS AI and machine learning services to ingest media, run inference across modalities, and surface findings to human classifiers in a structured, reviewable format. The session highlights how the team navigated the practical complexities of working with sensitive content, integrating model outputs with the BBFC's existing classification frameworks, and ensuring that the solution reflects the organization's editorial standards.

### Outcomes and Lessons for Legacy Organizations

The collaboration illustrates how a historic, mission-driven organization can evolve by pairing deep domain expertise with modern cloud-based AI capabilities. The resulting solution positions the BBFC to scale its classification work, share its expertise more broadly, and continue serving UK audiences in an era of rapidly expanding media consumption. More broadly, the session offers a blueprint for other established institutions: treat archival data as training fuel, adopt multimodal approaches when content is inherently multimodal, and design AI systems that augment rather than displace the human judgment at the heart of the mission.
