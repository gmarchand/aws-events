# CBS Sports Digital: Building the Golazo Network in the Cloud

## Overview of the Session

This AWS re:Invent 2023 session (MAE202) explores how CBS Sports Digital designed, built, and launched the Golazo Network, a dedicated channel for domestic and international soccer coverage that operates entirely in the cloud. The presentation moves beyond the typical direct-to-consumer streaming narrative and instead examines an end-to-end broadcast workflow—covering contribution, master control, playout, and distribution—all executed using cloud-based infrastructure on AWS. The discussion frames the project as a real-world example of how traditional broadcasting and digital streaming are converging, with cloud technology serving as the connective foundation.

## Cloud-Based Broadcast Architecture

### Reimagining the Traditional Broadcast Workflow

CBS Sports Digital approached the Golazo Network as more than a standard cloud FAST (free ad-supported streaming television) channel. The team architected a broadcast-grade pipeline in which live signal contribution from venues and feeds flows into the cloud, where master control operations and playout automation occur without reliance on traditional on-premises facilities. By centralizing these functions in AWS, CBS gained the agility to spin up channels, adapt to schedule changes, and manage international and domestic soccer rights coverage with significantly more flexibility than legacy broadcast plants would allow. The architecture supports both linear distribution and digital streaming outputs simultaneously, illustrating how a single cloud-native operation can feed multiple delivery models.

### Navigating Technical and Operational Challenges

The session details the obstacles CBS encountered in moving broadcast-critical workflows to the cloud, including ensuring low-latency contribution paths, maintaining the reliability standards expected of live linear television, integrating graphics and master control switching tools that historically lived in dedicated hardware, and orchestrating playout automation in a distributed environment. The team addressed these by combining AWS media services with partner technologies and by rethinking operational processes so that engineers and operators could manage a fully virtualized control room. Redundancy, monitoring, and failover strategies were emphasized as essential to delivering a continuous live programming experience.

## Strategic Outcomes and Industry Implications

### Convergence of Linear and Digital Broadcasting

The Golazo Network demonstrates how broadcasters can collapse the boundary between linear television channels and digital streaming products. Because the entire chain runs in the cloud, CBS can distribute the same content across cable, satellite, FAST platforms, and owned-and-operated streaming destinations from a unified origin, simplifying rights management and content packaging. This approach positions CBS to launch and evolve channels more rapidly, respond to audience demand around major soccer events, and reduce the capital expenditure traditionally associated with building new broadcast facilities.

### Lessons for Broadcasters Moving to the Cloud

The key takeaway from the session is that cloud-based broadcasting is now viable for full master-control and playout operations, not just for streaming distribution at the edge. CBS Sports Digital's experience offers a blueprint for media organizations evaluating similar transitions: start with a clear understanding of the broadcast requirements, partner closely with cloud and media technology providers, invest in operational training, and design for the convergence of linear and digital from day one. The Golazo Network stands as evidence that cloud technology has matured to support the most demanding live broadcast workflows while unlocking new operational and creative possibilities.
