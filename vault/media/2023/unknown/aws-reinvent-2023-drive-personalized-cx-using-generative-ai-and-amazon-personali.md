# Driving Personalized Customer Experiences with Generative AI and Amazon Personalize

## Session Overview

At AWS re:Invent 2023, session AIM225 explored how organizations can leverage generative AI in combination with Amazon Personalize to deliver hyper-personalized customer experiences across digital platforms such as e-commerce and streaming media. The session emphasized that traditional recommendation patterns—familiar taglines like "People who bought this also bought" or "Because you watched"—no longer satisfy modern consumer expectations. Today's customers demand experiences that reflect their individual tastes, behaviors, and contexts, and businesses that fail to deliver this level of personalization risk losing engagement and revenue.

The session featured insights from AWS specialists alongside FOX, a major AWS media customer, who shared real-world experiences applying these technologies at scale to engage audiences and drive business outcomes.

## Personalization in the Age of Generative AI

### The Shift Toward Hyper-Personalization

The presenters made the case that capturing and retaining customers in competitive digital markets depends on the ability to dynamically tailor every touchpoint—from product recommendations to promotional copy to content discovery. While machine learning has long powered recommendation engines, generative AI introduces a new dimension by enabling brands to create not only highly relevant suggestions but also compelling, customized narrative content around them. This means that the words, themes, and framing presented to each user can be generated in ways that resonate with that specific individual rather than relying on static templates.

### Capabilities of Amazon Personalize

Amazon Personalize was positioned as the foundational service for delivering this experience. Built on the same machine learning technology used by Amazon.com, the service provides ready-to-use recommenders, customizable recipes, and integration points that allow developers to embed personalization without deep ML expertise. New generative AI–powered capabilities extend Personalize beyond ranking and recommendation by helping brands generate engaging copy, themes, and contextual descriptions that wrap around recommended items, transforming a list of suggestions into a curated, story-driven experience for each user.

### FOX's Customer Experience Story

FOX shared how it applies these capabilities to deepen audience engagement across its media properties. By combining behavioral signals with Personalize and generative AI, FOX is able to surface content that matches viewer interests in the moment and present it through dynamically generated messaging that feels relevant and timely. The outcome is improved engagement metrics and stronger pathways to monetization, demonstrating that personalization at scale is both technically achievable and commercially impactful for media organizations.

## Key Takeaways and Outcomes

The session concluded with a clear directive for attendees: invest in personalization strategies that pair Amazon Personalize's ML-driven targeting with generative AI's ability to produce individualized content. Organizations that adopt this combined approach can move beyond generic recommendations toward experiences that feel uniquely crafted for each customer, ultimately driving higher engagement, retention, and revenue. FOX's example serves as validation that the technology is production-ready for demanding, large-scale customer-facing workloads, and AWS encouraged builders to explore Personalize's latest generative AI features to begin transforming their own customer experiences.
