# How ELF Reduced Their Carbon Footprint by Broadcasting on AWS

## Overview of the Session

At AWS re:Invent 2023, the European League of Football (ELF) presented their journey as a newly established sports league that adopted a fully cloud-based broadcasting production pipeline on AWS. Rather than investing in traditional on-premises broadcast infrastructure, ELF chose to build their entire production workflow in the cloud from inception. This decision allowed them to align their operations with modern sustainability principles while delivering professional-quality sports broadcasts across Europe. The session highlighted both the technical architecture and the measurable environmental benefits achieved during their 2023 season.

## Cloud-Based Broadcasting Transformation

### Building a Born-in-the-Cloud Production Pipeline

ELF leveraged AWS cloud-based remote production tools and infrastructure to replace what would traditionally require trucks, on-site control rooms, and extensive physical equipment at every stadium. By centralizing production in the cloud, the league eliminated the need to transport heavy broadcasting hardware to each venue, instead routing live feeds directly to AWS for remote production by distributed teams. This architectural shift fundamentally changed how a sports league of their scale could operate, enabling them to scale broadcasting capabilities without the capital expenditure typically associated with traditional media operations.

### Operational and Quality Improvements

The 2023 season demonstrated tangible benefits from this approach. ELF reported lower video latency, improved video quality, and increased reliability across their broadcasts compared to conventional production methods. Operational costs decreased significantly because the league no longer needed to deploy large crews and equipment to every match location. The cloud-native model also provided flexibility to scale resources up or down based on the broadcast schedule, ensuring the league only consumed compute and media resources when actually needed.

### Sustainability and Carbon Footprint Reduction

A central outcome of the migration was a measurable reduction in ELF's carbon footprint. By avoiding the transportation of broadcast trucks and equipment between stadiums and consolidating production in efficient AWS data centers, the league reduced fuel consumption, equipment manufacturing demand, and overall energy use. The session connected these results directly to the sustainability pillar of the AWS Well-Architected Framework, illustrating how architectural decisions—such as right-sizing instances, leveraging managed services, and minimizing idle resources—translate into environmental gains for media workloads.

## Key Takeaways and Guidance

The presenters offered practical methods for architecting media workloads efficiently in alignment with AWS Well-Architected sustainability principles. They emphasized that organizations building or migrating broadcast pipelines should evaluate workload placement, optimize encoding and transcoding processes, and adopt managed services that improve hardware utilization at the platform level. ELF's experience served as a concrete case study demonstrating that sustainability, cost efficiency, and broadcast quality are mutually reinforcing rather than competing goals. The session concluded with an invitation for other media organizations to consider AWS as a partner in their own sustainability journeys, using ELF's transformation as a blueprint for what is achievable when a league commits to cloud-first broadcasting from day one.
