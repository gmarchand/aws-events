# Considerations for Using an AWS Enterprise Discount Program

## Overview of the AWS Enterprise Discount Program

The AWS Enterprise Discount Program, commonly referred to as EDP, represents one of the most significant savings vehicles available to large AWS customers. Delivered as a lightning talk at AWS re:Invent 2023 by CloudKeeper, an AWS Partner, this session examines how enterprise customers can evaluate whether an EDP aligns with their cloud spending strategy. The program offers customized discounts in exchange for a multi-year spending commitment, making it an attractive but consequential decision that requires careful analysis before signing.

## Weighing the Advantages Against the Trade-offs

### Benefits and Drawbacks of Committing to an EDP

The primary advantage of an EDP lies in the meaningful percentage discount applied across qualifying AWS spend, which can translate to substantial savings for enterprises with predictable, growing cloud usage. Customers also gain enhanced relationships with AWS, including additional support and credits in some cases. However, these benefits come with significant trade-offs. Customers must commit to a specific annual spending threshold over the contract term, and any shortfall typically results in the customer still owing the committed amount. This rigidity can become problematic if business circumstances change, workloads are optimized more aggressively than expected, or migration plans shift. The session emphasizes that organizations must honestly assess their growth trajectory and cloud maturity before locking into multi-year obligations.

### Determining the Right Commitment Amount and Duration

A central theme of the talk is the importance of correctly sizing the commitment. Committing too aggressively risks penalties or wasted spend, while committing too conservatively leaves savings on the table. Customers should analyze historical usage patterns, forecast future workloads with realistic assumptions about optimization efforts, and account for variability in projects, mergers, or divestitures. The duration of the agreement also matters significantly, as longer terms generally yield deeper discounts but reduce flexibility to adapt to changing technology and business priorities.

### Maximizing Benefits While Preserving Flexibility

The presentation stresses that signing an EDP is not the end of cost optimization but the beginning of a disciplined management practice. Customers can maximize value by continuing to apply Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, and architectural optimizations on top of EDP discounts, ensuring stacked savings rather than treating the EDP as a substitute for good cost hygiene. CloudKeeper introduces its EDP+ offering as a way for customers to extend gains and retain flexibility, positioning partner-led approaches as a means to reduce commitment risk while still capturing enterprise-level discounts.

## Key Takeaways for Enterprise Customers

The overarching message is that an EDP can deliver strong financial outcomes, but only when entered into with rigorous forecasting, ongoing optimization, and a clear understanding of contractual obligations. Enterprises are encouraged to model multiple scenarios, negotiate terms aligned with realistic growth, and consider partner solutions such as CloudKeeper EDP+ when additional flexibility or expertise is needed. Ultimately, the decision should be driven by a balanced view of savings potential, operational agility, and long-term cloud strategy.
