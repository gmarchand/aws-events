# ULA Transforms Rocket Development Efforts with AWS

## Overview of the Session

At AWS re:Invent 2023, United Launch Alliance (ULA), a leading spacecraft launch service provider, presented its strategic transformation initiative aimed at dramatically increasing launch cadence to meet rising customer demand, most notably from Amazon's Project Kuiper satellite constellation. The session detailed how ULA partnered with AWS to fundamentally rethink its IT foundation, moving from a traditional on-premises infrastructure model to a cloud-native architecture capable of supporting continuous, around-the-clock launch operations. Throughout the discussion, ULA leaders emphasized that the shift to AWS is not merely an infrastructure modernization effort but a core enabler of the company's ability to scale its mission-critical engineering and operations workflows.

## Building a Modern Cloud Foundation for Launch Operations

### Establishing a Common Landing Zone

ULA worked closely with AWS to design and implement a common landing zone that serves as the backbone for 24/7 launch operations. This standardized environment provides the governance, security guardrails, and operational consistency required to support a high-tempo launch business where reliability is non-negotiable. By centralizing account structures, identity management, networking, and compliance controls, ULA established a scalable platform on which engineering teams can rapidly deploy new workloads without compromising on safety or auditability. The landing zone also enables ULA to extend cloud capabilities consistently across its enterprise, ensuring that every team operates within the same secure, well-architected framework.

### Migrating a Portfolio of More Than 400 Applications

A central pillar of the transformation involved migrating an application suite of over 400 workloads from on-premises data centers to AWS. The migration was driven by the goal of achieving 99.9% reliability across the application portfolio, a critical requirement given the role these systems play in supporting rocket design, manufacturing, testing, and launch operations. By moving to AWS, ULA gained immediate improvements in resiliency through multi-Availability Zone deployments, in scalability through elastic compute and storage, and in security through native AWS controls and continuous monitoring. The migration also reduced operational overhead, allowing ULA's IT teams to focus on higher-value engineering enablement rather than data center maintenance.

## Accelerating Engineering with Container-Based Capabilities

### Modernizing Software Engineering for Mission Success

Beyond lifting and shifting existing workloads, ULA invested in developing container-based software engineering capabilities on AWS to expand its mission success testing capacity. Containerization gives ULA's engineers a portable, repeatable, and highly automated environment for building, testing, and validating the software that controls and supports launch missions. This approach increases throughput on mission-critical testing pipelines, shortens iteration cycles, and improves confidence in the software systems that ultimately fly on ULA rockets. The combination of standardized container platforms with AWS managed services allows ULA to run more concurrent tests, catch issues earlier in development, and respond more quickly to evolving mission requirements.

### Supporting Increased Launch Cadence and Project Kuiper

The cumulative effect of these initiatives is a significantly higher launch cadence, which is essential for ULA to meet its commitments to customers including Amazon's Project Kuiper. Deploying a broadband satellite constellation of Kuiper's scale requires a sustained tempo of launches that traditional ULA infrastructure was not designed to support. By leveraging AWS for both back-office applications and engineering workloads, ULA can scale operations elastically, harden reliability, and accelerate the pace at which it prepares vehicles and payloads for flight.

## Outcomes and Strategic Impact

ULA's collaboration with AWS has delivered measurable improvements in resiliency, scalability, security, and operational efficiency across the enterprise. The transition from on-premises operations to a cloud-based model has positioned ULA to operate continuously, support a growing manifest of high-profile missions, and innovate more rapidly in software and systems engineering. The session concluded by reinforcing that this transformation is foundational to ULA's future, providing the digital infrastructure required to launch more frequently, more reliably, and at the scale demanded by next-generation space programs.
