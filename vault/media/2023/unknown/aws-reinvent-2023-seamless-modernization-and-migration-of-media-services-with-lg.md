# Seamless Modernization and Migration of Media Services with LG U+

## Overview of the Session

This AWS re:Invent 2023 session (PRO101) explores how LG U+, one of South Korea's leading telecommunications providers, partnered with AWS Professional Services to modernize and migrate its IPTV media services to the cloud without disrupting customer experience. The session presents the strategic framework, architectural patterns, and operational best practices that enabled a smooth cutover from legacy on-premises systems to a modern, scalable AWS-based platform. Attendees learn how thoughtful planning combined with proven migration methodologies can transform mission-critical media workloads while maintaining service continuity.

## Strategy and Execution of the Modernization Journey

### Defining the Migration Approach

LG U+ and AWS Professional Services began by carefully evaluating the existing IPTV media services to determine the appropriate modernization strategy. Rather than taking a single uniform approach, the teams identified workloads suited for re-platforming and others that benefited from full re-architecture into cloud-native services. This assessment phase allowed the project to balance speed, risk, and long-term value, ensuring that each component of the media services landscape received the treatment best aligned with its technical and business requirements. The collaboration emphasized rigorous discovery, dependency mapping, and prioritization to build a migration roadmap grounded in the realities of a large telecom operator delivering television services to millions of subscribers.

### Applying Best Practices for Zero-Disruption Cutover

A central theme of the session is how the team achieved a seamless cutover with zero service disruption. The presenters describe key patterns such as parallel run environments, traffic shifting through controlled rollouts, and robust validation gates that compared legacy and modernized service outputs before redirecting customer traffic. Observability and automated rollback mechanisms played a critical role, allowing engineers to detect anomalies quickly and revert if necessary. By treating the migration as an iterative, low-risk process rather than a single high-stakes event, LG U+ minimized exposure to failures while progressively retiring legacy infrastructure.

### Modernizing the Media Services Architecture

Beyond migration mechanics, the session highlights how the IPTV platform was reshaped using AWS services to gain elasticity, operational efficiency, and faster innovation cycles. Modernization brought benefits such as improved scalability to handle peak viewing demand, simplified operations through managed services, and a foundation for future feature development. The collaboration between LG U+ engineering teams and AWS Professional Services ensured knowledge transfer throughout the engagement, equipping LG U+ with the skills and patterns needed to continue evolving the platform independently.

## Key Outcomes and Takeaways

The LG U+ case study demonstrates that even complex, customer-facing media services can be migrated and modernized without service interruption when guided by a clear strategy and disciplined execution. The most important lessons center on the value of upfront discovery, the use of parallel-run and incremental cutover patterns, comprehensive validation and observability, and close partnership between customer engineering teams and AWS specialists. For organizations contemplating similar transformations, the session underscores that modernization is not solely a technology exercise but a carefully orchestrated program that aligns architecture decisions with business continuity requirements, ultimately delivering a more agile and resilient platform for future growth.
