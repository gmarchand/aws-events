# How Amazon MGM Studios Protects Its Most Important Data

## Overview of the Challenge

Amazon MGM Studios operates at the forefront of the media and entertainment industry, managing exabytes of content that represent some of the most valuable intellectual property in the world. As a leading content creator, the studio must support the diverse workflow needs of production, marketing, technology, and security teams while simultaneously safeguarding its creative assets from threats. The scale of this challenge is significant: the studio routinely handles petabytes of content transfers flowing in from a global network of partners, vendors, and production collaborators. This constant movement of high-value data demands a security approach that protects intellectual property without becoming a bottleneck for creative teams working under tight production deadlines.

This session, presented by AWS Partner Trend Micro alongside PacGenesis, explores how Amazon MGM Studios built a scalable security framework on AWS to validate and protect content as it moves through its ecosystem.

## Building a Scalable Content Security Framework

### Securing High-Volume Content Transfers

The studio's security strategy centers on ensuring that every piece of incoming content is scanned, validated, and verified before it touches production environments. Working with PacGenesis for high-speed file transfer capabilities and Trend Micro for advanced threat detection, Amazon MGM Studios established an automated pipeline that inspects content at the point of ingestion. This integration allows the studio to maintain the velocity required by modern media production workflows while ensuring that malware, suspicious payloads, and unauthorized files are intercepted before they can compromise downstream systems. The architecture leverages AWS-native services to scale elastically with content volume, eliminating the need for manual intervention even as transfer demands fluctuate dramatically across production cycles.

### Balancing Operational Excellence with Speed

A core theme of the discussion is that security cannot come at the cost of slowing creative work. The studio's approach treats operational excellence in data security as a competitive enabler rather than a friction point. By embedding Trend Micro's scanning capabilities directly into the content transfer workflow managed by PacGenesis, security checks happen in line with delivery rather than as a separate, time-consuming step. This design ensures that production, marketing, and technology teams receive validated content quickly, while security teams gain confidence that intellectual property remains protected throughout its lifecycle on AWS.

### Protecting Intellectual Property at Scale

Given the exabyte-scale footprint of Amazon MGM Studios' content library, the partnership emphasizes layered protection that extends beyond simple file scanning. The combined solution provides visibility into content provenance, automated policy enforcement, and continuous validation across the studio's AWS environment. This holistic posture allows the studio to confidently collaborate with external vendors and partners while maintaining strict control over its most sensitive assets, including unreleased films, series, and proprietary production data.

## Outcomes and Key Takeaways

The collaboration between Amazon MGM Studios, Trend Micro, and PacGenesis demonstrates that large-scale media organizations can achieve robust data protection without sacrificing the agility their business demands. By leveraging AWS Partner solutions designed for media-scale workloads, the studio has built a security model that automatically scales with content volume, validates incoming files without disrupting workflows, and provides the operational excellence needed to safeguard exabytes of irreplaceable creative work. The session underscores a broader lesson for the media and entertainment industry: protecting intellectual property at scale requires purpose-built partnerships and automation-first thinking, where security becomes an invisible but essential layer woven into every stage of content creation and distribution.
