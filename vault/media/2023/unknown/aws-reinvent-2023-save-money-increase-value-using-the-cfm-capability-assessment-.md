# Saving Money and Increasing Value with the AWS CFM Capability Assessment

## Overview of Cloud Financial Management Maturity

Cloud Financial Management (CFM) has emerged as a critical discipline for organizations seeking to maximize the value of their AWS investments. AWS research demonstrates a clear correlation between CFM maturity and financial outcomes: customers with more mature cloud financial management practices consistently achieve greater savings on their AWS bills and unlock more meaningful business value from their cloud operations. This session, presented at AWS re:Invent 2023, introduces the CFM Capability Assessment as a structured tool designed to help customers understand where they stand on this maturity curve and chart a path toward improvement.

The central premise is that cost optimization in the cloud is not simply a matter of turning off unused resources or buying reserved capacity. Instead, it requires a holistic set of organizational capabilities spanning visibility, accountability, optimization practices, and cultural alignment around financial responsibility. Without these foundational capabilities in place, individual cost-saving tactics yield only short-term results.

## The CFM Capability Assessment as a Discovery Tool

### Purpose and Methodology

The CFM Capability Assessment functions as a discovery tool that evaluates an organization's cloud financial management practices across multiple dimensions. Rather than providing a generic checklist, the assessment examines the unique strengths and weaknesses of each company, recognizing that every organization arrives at its cloud journey with different starting points, business pressures, and operational structures. By systematically working through the assessment, customers gain a clear picture of which CFM capabilities are well-developed and which require investment.

The output of the assessment is an optimization roadmap tailored to the company's specific circumstances. This roadmap prioritizes improvements based on where the greatest gains can be achieved relative to the organization's current maturity, helping leaders focus their efforts and resources on initiatives that will deliver the most impact.

### Benchmarking Against Other AWS Customers

A particularly valuable feature of the Capability Assessment is its benchmarking functionality. Customers can compare their CFM maturity against that of other AWS customers, providing an external reference point that contextualizes internal performance. This comparative view helps organizations understand whether they are leading, lagging, or aligned with peers, and it surfaces aspirational practices adopted by more mature customers. Benchmarking also strengthens the business case for CFM investment by providing data-driven evidence to support conversations with finance leaders, executives, and other stakeholders.

## Driving Outcomes Through Structured Improvement

The session emphasizes that CFM maturity is a journey rather than a destination. By using the Capability Assessment as a recurring diagnostic, organizations can track their progress over time, validate the impact of their investments in people, processes, and tooling, and continuously refine their roadmap as cloud usage evolves. The ultimate goal is to move beyond reactive cost management toward proactive financial governance that enables faster innovation, more confident scaling, and stronger alignment between cloud spending and business outcomes. Customers who embrace this structured approach are positioned to capture both immediate savings on their AWS bills and longer-term strategic value from their cloud investments.
