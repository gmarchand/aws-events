# AWS at NAB 2023: Making Global Pictures and Telling Stories with Untold Studios

## Overview of the Fireside Chat

At NAB 2023, AWS hosted a fireside conversation featuring Michael Wolfson, Chief Operating Officer of Untold Studios, and Sam Reid, the studio's Director of Technology, alongside Gretchen Libby of AWS. The discussion centered on how Untold Studios has built an entirely cloud-native creative production company, leveraging AWS infrastructure to support its award-winning visual effects, animation, and immersive storytelling work. The conversation explored the studio's creative philosophy, its technology choices, and its strategy for scaling globally in an industry traditionally bound by physical workstations and on-premises render farms.

## Untold Studios and Its Creative Mission

### Building a Cloud-Native Creative Studio

Untold Studios distinguishes itself by operating without a traditional on-premises infrastructure, having designed its production pipeline around AWS from the outset. Michael Wolfson described the studio's mission as one of empowering artists and storytellers to focus on craft rather than be constrained by hardware limitations or geographic boundaries. By eliminating the friction of physical workstations and localized rendering, the studio enables creative teams to collaborate across disciplines — including visual effects, animation, design, and music — within a unified digital environment. This approach has supported the delivery of high-profile, award-winning campaigns and content for global brands and entertainment clients.

### Recent Award-Winning Work

The studio's recent output highlights its ability to combine technical excellence with bold creative vision. Wolfson and Reid pointed to projects that span commercial work, episodic content, and experiential pieces, each demonstrating how a cloud-first model accelerates iteration and expands the creative possibilities available to directors and artists. The flexibility to spin up resources on demand has allowed Untold to take on ambitious projects that would otherwise require significant capital investment in fixed infrastructure.

## Cloud as a Creative Enabler

### Technology Choices and Workflow

Sam Reid detailed how AWS services underpin the studio's day-to-day operations, from virtual workstations that allow artists to work from anywhere, to scalable rendering capacity that flexes with project demands. By treating compute and storage as elastic resources, Untold avoids the bottlenecks common in traditional VFX pipelines, where deadlines often collide with hardware constraints. The cloud-native approach also simplifies onboarding new talent and integrating freelancers, since access to tools and assets is delivered through secure, centralized systems rather than shipped hardware.

### Global Expansion in a Digital-First World

A central theme of the discussion was how the cloud has made global expansion practical for a studio of Untold's size. Wolfson explained that operating in a digital-first world means the studio can recruit talent and serve clients across regions without replicating expensive physical infrastructure in every location. This model lowers the barriers to opening new offices and enables seamless collaboration between teams in different cities and time zones. The decision to build on AWS was therefore strategic as well as technical — it positions Untold to grow into new markets quickly while maintaining a consistent creative standard.

## Key Outcomes and Takeaways

The conversation reinforced that cloud infrastructure is no longer a supporting utility for creative studios but a core enabler of artistic ambition and business agility. Untold Studios serves as a clear example of how a digital-first, AWS-backed foundation allows a creative company to compete globally, attract top talent regardless of location, and deliver complex work at scale. The fireside chat closed with reflections on the future of storytelling, where the boundaries between disciplines continue to blur and where the cloud will remain central to enabling artists to realize increasingly ambitious visions.
