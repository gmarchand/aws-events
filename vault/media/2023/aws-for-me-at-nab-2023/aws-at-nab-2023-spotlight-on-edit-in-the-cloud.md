# AWS at NAB 2023: Spotlight on Edit in the Cloud

## Overview of the Cloud-Based Studio Workflow

At NAB Show 2023, AWS presented a comprehensive demonstration of its end-to-end studio in the cloud workflow, with a particular emphasis on edit in the cloud capabilities. The showcase illustrated how modern content production can be reimagined by leveraging cloud infrastructure at every stage, from initial capture through final post-production. By moving traditionally on-premises workflows to AWS, production teams gain the ability to collaborate across geographies and scale resources on demand, addressing some of the most pressing challenges in contemporary media and entertainment.

## Capture, Ingest, and Editorial Integration

The workflow begins on a virtual set, where content is captured and uploaded directly to AWS rather than being stored on physical media that must be transported and ingested manually. Once the content lands in the cloud, it becomes immediately accessible through partner integrations that connect AWS storage and services to editorial workstations. This tight integration with established media partners ensures that editors can work within the tools they already know, while benefiting from cloud-native ingest and asset management. The result is a significant reduction in the lag between production and post-production, enabling editorial work to begin almost as soon as footage is captured.

### GPU-Powered Virtual Editing

A central highlight of the demonstration was the use of GPU-powered virtual workstations on AWS, which allow editors to perform demanding tasks with the same responsiveness they would expect from high-end local hardware. These cloud workstations support a seamless virtual editing experience, giving creative teams the flexibility to work from anywhere without sacrificing performance. By combining capture, ingest, partner-driven editorial tooling, and GPU-accelerated workstations into a single connected pipeline, AWS positioned its studio in the cloud offering as a practical foundation for the next generation of virtual production and remote post-production workflows.
