# AWS at NAB 2023: Content Production Overview

## Showcasing an End-to-End Studio in the Cloud

At the NAB Show 2023, AWS presented a comprehensive demonstration of how modern filmmaking can be reimagined entirely within a cloud-based environment. The centerpiece of the showcase was a short film titled *Race to the Cloud*, which served as a practical case study illustrating how every stage of content production can be executed using AWS infrastructure and services. Rather than presenting cloud capabilities as isolated features, AWS used the film's creation to demonstrate a unified, production-ready pipeline that media and entertainment customers can adopt today.

The booth experience emphasized the interconnected nature of the workflow, highlighting how AWS compute services combined with AWS Media Services—particularly for uncompressed video transport—enable studios to break free from the constraints of traditional on-premises infrastructure. The overarching message centered on speed and creative agility: with cloud-based tools, content creators can iterate faster, collaborate across geographies, and scale resources dynamically to meet the demands of complex productions.

## Production and Post-Production Workflows

### Pre-Production and Principal Photography

The demonstration began at the earliest stages of filmmaking, featuring a virtual art department where designers and creatives collaborate in the cloud to develop visual concepts and assets. This flowed directly into principal photography, which incorporated virtual production techniques. By integrating cloud-based tools with on-set workflows, AWS illustrated how filmmakers can blend physical and digital environments seamlessly, reducing reliance on traditional location shoots and accelerating decision-making during the shoot itself.

### Post-Production and Finishing

The post-production showcase covered the full breadth of finishing tasks that historically have required specialized workstations and tightly controlled facilities. Animation and rendering workflows ran on cloud compute, demonstrating the elasticity needed for compute-intensive visual effects work. Picture and sound editorial were performed remotely, with editors accessing high-quality media without local copies. Color grading and quality control workflows likewise operated in the cloud, proving that even the most color-critical and detail-sensitive tasks can be performed reliably through cloud-based pipelines. Together, these demonstrations confirmed that no stage of post-production needs to remain tethered to physical infrastructure.

## Implications for Media and Entertainment Customers

The overall takeaway from AWS at NAB 2023 was that a fully cloud-based studio is no longer aspirational but operationally viable. By stitching together compute, media transport, and creative tools into one coherent environment, AWS provided a blueprint for studios seeking to modernize. Customers can expect benefits in collaboration, scalability, and time-to-delivery, while also gaining the flexibility to assemble distributed creative teams. *Race to the Cloud* ultimately served as proof that cloud-native filmmaking is ready for mainstream adoption across the content production industry.
