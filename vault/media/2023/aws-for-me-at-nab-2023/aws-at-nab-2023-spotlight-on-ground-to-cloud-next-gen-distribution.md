# AWS at NAB 2023: Spotlight on Ground to Cloud and Next-Generation Distribution

## Overview of the AWS Demonstration at NAB Show 2023

At the National Association of Broadcasters (NAB) Show 2023, AWS presented a comprehensive demonstration of how cloud technology can transform the entire broadcast production lifecycle. The showcase brought together AWS's media and entertainment portfolio to illustrate a complete end-to-end broadcast workflow operating in the cloud, moving beyond traditional on-premises production infrastructure. The presentation aimed to demonstrate to broadcasters, producers, and media professionals how AWS solutions can simplify event production while enabling seamless content distribution at scale.

To make the demonstration tangible and interactive, AWS simulated a live soccer event directly at the show venue. Attendees became active participants by taking shots on goal using a specially designed sensor-equipped soccer ball, transforming a typical conference booth into a functioning live sports broadcast environment.

## Connected Ball Technology and Live Capture

### Sensor-Driven Ball Tracking with Machine Learning

The centerpiece of the experience was the connected ball, embedded with sensors that captured detailed performance data with every kick. Powered by AWS machine learning capabilities, the technology delivered unprecedented real-time insight into ball movement, including trajectory, speed, and other performance metrics. This level of granular data illustrated how machine learning can elevate sports storytelling by surfacing analytics that were previously difficult or impossible to capture in real time.

### Remote-Operated Cameras and Private 5G Connectivity

Surrounding the simulated pitch, multiple remote-operated cameras captured each shot from various angles, mirroring the production setup of a professional sporting event. The live feeds were transported using ground-to-cloud technologies, with a private 5G network serving as a critical enabler for low-latency, high-bandwidth connectivity between the field of play and the cloud-based production environment. This configuration demonstrated how broadcasters can reduce reliance on heavy on-site infrastructure while maintaining broadcast-grade reliability and performance.

## Cloud Production and Personalized Distribution

The captured footage was processed through AWS broadcast services to deliver the experience as a live broadcast, demonstrating that cloud-based production can match the responsiveness expected of traditional live workflows. Participants were able to view replays of their shots and receive personalized highlight reels accompanied by individual statistics generated from the connected ball data. This personalized output highlighted the potential for fan engagement, customized content delivery, and next-generation distribution models that combine live production with tailored, data-driven storytelling.

By unifying connected device data, machine learning, private 5G, remote camera operation, cloud production, and content distribution within a single demonstration, AWS reinforced its message that cloud technology can streamline complex broadcast operations and unlock new creative and commercial possibilities for the media and entertainment industry.
