# From Broadcast to Every Screen: How AWS Partners Are Redefining Vertical Video with AI

## The Shift in Audience Consumption and the Vertical Video Challenge

Audience behavior has undergone a fundamental transformation, with Gen-Z viewers discovering and consuming content predominantly through vertical-format platforms such as TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Broadcasters who fail to deliver content in these formats are rapidly losing relevance and market share to creators and competitors who natively meet audiences where they already are. However, producing vertical video at scale has historically posed a significant operational burden for traditional video teams, requiring expensive post-production pipelines, additional staffing, and often resulting in missed opportunities to capitalize on viral moments while they are still culturally relevant.

This session, hosted by an AWS Elemental product leader alongside executives from ScorePlay, Tagboard, and Grabyo, addresses how AWS partners are solving this challenge by embedding artificial intelligence directly into the live video workflow rather than treating it as an afterthought.

## AWS Elemental Inference and the "Process Once, Optimize Everywhere" Model

### Real-Time AI Integration with Live Encoding

The central innovation discussed is AWS Elemental Inference, a service that applies AI in parallel with live video encoding rather than as a post-production step. By integrating intelligence into the encoding stage itself, broadcasters can automatically generate vertical video outputs and highlight clips in real time, achieving latencies as low as three to ten seconds. Critically, the service is designed so that customers do not need in-house AI expertise to benefit from it, lowering the barrier to adoption for video teams already stretched thin.

The architectural philosophy underpinning Elemental Inference is "process once, optimize everywhere." Rather than running multiple parallel workflows for each output format and distribution channel, broadcasters can ingest and process content a single time and then automatically derive the various vertical, horizontal, and clipped outputs needed for diverse platforms. This approach reduces compute costs, simplifies operational complexity, and accelerates time to publish.

### Partner Implementations and Use Cases

ScorePlay, Tagboard, and Grabyo each demonstrate how Elemental Inference is being woven into their respective product offerings to unlock new customer value. The discussion highlights sports highlight automation, where key moments are detected and clipped without manual intervention; content amplification across multiple distribution endpoints; and direct social media publishing pipelines that allow rights holders to reach audiences within seconds of a live moment occurring. By embedding the service into existing platforms, these partners enable their customers to adopt AI-driven vertical workflows without rebuilding the production systems they already depend on.

## Outcomes and the AI-First Media Landscape

The collective takeaway from the session is that the integration of AI into live video infrastructure is no longer optional for broadcasters seeking to remain competitive in an AI-first media environment. Through partnerships with ScorePlay, Tagboard, and Grabyo, AWS Elemental Inference allows media organizations to reach new and younger audiences on the platforms they prefer, dramatically reduce production costs and headcount requirements, and capture viral moments while they still carry cultural weight. The session concludes that the future of broadcasting lies in workflows where intelligence is built into the pipeline from the outset, enabling content to flow seamlessly from a single source to every screen and format the modern audience demands.
