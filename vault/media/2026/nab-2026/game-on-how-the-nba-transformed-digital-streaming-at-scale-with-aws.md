# Game On: How the NBA Transformed Digital Streaming at Scale with AWS

## Overview of the NBA's Streaming Transformation

The National Basketball Association undertook a major modernization of its digital streaming infrastructure by migrating its workflows to Amazon Web Services. This transition represented a strategic move designed to support the league's growing global audience, expanding content portfolio, and increasingly sophisticated viewer expectations. By embracing a cloud-native architecture, the NBA positioned itself to deliver consistent, high-quality streaming experiences across a calendar that spans Summer League, the regular season, All-Star Weekend, and the playoffs.

To execute this transformation, the NBA partnered with the Unified Operations for Media (UOM) team at AWS, leveraging their specialized expertise in live streaming, broadcast workflows, and large-scale media delivery. This collaboration combined the NBA's deep understanding of fan engagement with AWS's media-focused engineering capabilities, allowing the league to rebuild its streaming foundation on a platform purpose-built for the demands of professional sports content.

## Building a Scalable Streaming Platform on AWS

### Architecting for Live Sports at Global Scale

The migration centered on creating a streaming platform capable of handling the unique challenges of live sports, where unpredictable audience surges, low-latency requirements, and uncompromising reliability all converge. The NBA worked with AWS to design workflows that could ingest, process, and distribute live game feeds while maintaining broadcast-grade quality. The architecture emphasized elasticity, enabling the platform to scale automatically from regular season games to marquee events that draw dramatically larger concurrent audiences.

### Delivering Seamless Experiences Across the Season

A core objective of the project was ensuring that fans received a consistently smooth viewing experience regardless of when or how they tuned in. The platform launched in time for Summer League Opening Day, serving as an early proving ground before scaling up to support the full regular season. By the time All-Star Weekend arrived, the system had been refined to handle one of the league's highest-profile content moments, validating the resilience of the AWS-based workflows. This phased rollout allowed the NBA and UOM team to identify optimizations and harden the platform progressively.

### Partnership and Operational Excellence

The collaboration with the Unified Operations for Media team proved central to the project's success. UOM provided the streaming media expertise needed to translate the NBA's broadcast standards into cloud-native workflows, while ongoing operational partnership ensured that the platform remained dependable through high-stakes live events. This combined approach of technology migration and operational support gave the NBA a foundation that not only met current demands but is positioned to evolve with future innovations in digital streaming.

## Outcomes and Forward Momentum

The migration delivered a modernized streaming platform that has supported the NBA through some of its most demanding broadcast moments, demonstrating that cloud infrastructure can meet the stringent requirements of premier live sports. Beyond the immediate operational improvements, the project established a flexible foundation that the NBA can build upon to introduce new features, expand global reach, and experiment with emerging streaming technologies. The session highlighted how strategic partnerships and purpose-built media services on AWS can transform legacy streaming operations into agile, scalable systems ready for the future of sports entertainment.
