# AI-Native Prime Video: Transforming Live Ops, Media Management, and Broadcast Scheduling

## Strategic Vision for 2026

Prime Video is executing an ambitious AI-native strategy targeting full operational transformation by 2026. Rather than treating artificial intelligence as a peripheral enhancement, the organization is embedding Amazon QuickSuite and Kiro directly into the workflows that power live sports broadcasting, media management, and broadcast scheduling. This shift positions Prime Video to operate with greater speed, accuracy, and scale across its global content delivery operations, while empowering teams to focus on creative and editorial decisions rather than repetitive operational tasks.

The transformation centers on two complementary platforms working in tandem. Amazon QuickSuite provides the agent-based intelligence layer that surfaces institutional knowledge and automates complex workflows, while Kiro serves as the development accelerator that allows teams to prototype, build, and deploy new tooling rapidly. Together, these technologies form the backbone of a more responsive and intelligent operational environment.

## Key Operational Initiatives

### Media Management Assistant Powered by QuickSuite

A central pillar of the strategy is the Media Management Assistant, built on QuickSuite agents to streamline sports media workflows. The assistant gives operations teams immediate access to comprehensive internal knowledge bases, transforming how users locate documentation, retrieve operational resources, and resolve questions during time-sensitive live events. By consolidating fragmented internal knowledge into a single conversational interface, the assistant reduces the friction operators face when navigating the complex landscape of sports broadcasting requirements, partner specifications, and internal procedures. This translates directly into faster decision-making during live productions where seconds matter.

### Accelerated Development with Kiro

Kiro is being deployed to compress the development cycle for media management initiatives. Engineering teams use Kiro to accelerate code generation and rapidly prototype new capabilities, enabling them to move from concept to working solution at a pace that traditional development workflows cannot match. Crucially, these prototypes are tested and deployed directly within Prime Video's live production environment, creating a tight feedback loop between operational needs and engineering output. This approach allows the organization to iterate on broadcast scheduling tools and live operations features in close alignment with the realities of running global sports events.

### Integrated Live Operations and Broadcast Scheduling

The combined application of QuickSuite and Kiro extends across live operations and broadcast scheduling, ensuring that the intelligence layer and the development layer reinforce each other. Live ops teams benefit from agent-driven assistance during broadcasts, while scheduling workflows gain new automation built rapidly through Kiro-enabled prototyping. The result is an operational ecosystem where AI is not a single feature but a connective tissue running through every major workflow.

## Outcomes and Forward Momentum

By aligning agent-based assistance with rapid development capabilities, Prime Video is establishing a model in which operational expertise, engineering velocity, and AI tooling converge. The organization expects measurable gains in productivity, faster onboarding for complex sports workflows, and a sustainable foundation for continued innovation. As 2026 approaches, these initiatives mark a decisive move from experimentation toward AI as the default operating mode for Prime Video's most critical workflows.
