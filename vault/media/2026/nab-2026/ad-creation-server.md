# Ad Creation Server: Empowering SMBs Through Agentic AI

## Bridging the Gap Between Small Businesses and Television Advertising

Media publishers and advertising platforms have long struggled to serve small and medium businesses (SMBs) that lack the production expertise and substantial budgets traditionally required to create television advertisements. This demonstration addresses that gap by showcasing an ad creation server that enables business owners to generate professional-quality advertisements through a simple, conversational interface. Rather than navigating complex production workflows, business owners can describe their brand identity and marketing goals in natural language, while publishers contribute contextual information about where the ad will appear. This collaborative approach ensures that finished ads are precisely tailored to specific placement slots, dramatically reducing creative costs and removing barriers that historically excluded smaller advertisers from premium media inventory.

## The Agentic AI Workflow Behind the Solution

### Technical Architecture and Standards

The solution relies on an agentic AI workflow built on Amazon Bedrock and powered by Amazon Nova models, which orchestrate the creative process from initial concept through final delivery. By leveraging AdCP open agentic advertising standards, the system ensures interoperability across the advertising ecosystem and aligns with emerging industry conventions for AI-driven ad operations. The agent guides users step by step, interpreting their descriptions, incorporating publisher-supplied placement context, and producing a completed advertisement in a matter of minutes rather than weeks.

### Business Outcomes and Revenue Opportunities

The strategic outcome of this approach is the unlocking of a previously inaccessible SMB advertising market for media companies. By lowering the cost and complexity of ad creation, publishers can attract customers who would otherwise direct their advertising spend to digital platforms or forgo television advertising entirely. This creates new revenue streams for media organizations while giving small businesses access to professional television advertising that was previously out of reach, ultimately expanding the overall advertising market and strengthening publisher economics.
