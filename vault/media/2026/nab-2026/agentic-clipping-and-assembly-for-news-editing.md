# Agentic Clipping and Assembly for News Editing

## Accelerating Newsroom Production with AWS Generative AI

News organizations operate under relentless deadline pressure, requiring fast identification, curation, and assembly of footage for breaking stories. Traditional workflows demand significant manual effort from editors who must search through vast media libraries, review clips, and stitch together coherent narratives within shrinking timeframes. AWS addresses this operational bottleneck by combining Amazon Bedrock and Amazon Nova foundation models with agentic AI workflows, transforming how journalists move from raw assets to broadcast-ready content.

## Intelligent Content Analysis and Automated Assembly

### Multimodal Understanding Through Amazon Nova

The solution leverages Amazon Nova's multimodal capabilities to interpret both written news scripts and the visual and audio content of existing footage. By analyzing scripts alongside video assets simultaneously, the foundation model identifies semantic relationships between narrative beats and available media, surfacing the clips most contextually relevant to each segment of a developing story. This deep understanding extends beyond keyword matching, allowing the system to grasp tone, subject matter, and editorial intent.

### Agentic Timeline Generation

Building on this analysis, agentic AI orchestrates the assembly process by generating recommended rough-cut timelines. Rather than presenting editors with isolated clip suggestions, the agents construct sequenced timelines that align with the script's structure, producing a starting point that editors can refine instead of build from scratch. This shifts human effort away from low-value asset management toward higher-value storytelling and editorial judgment.

### Integration with Partner Solutions

The workflow integrates with AWS Partner offerings from Fonn Group, specifically the Mimir media asset management platform and the Saga editorial system. This integration ensures that the agentic capabilities operate within the tools news teams already use, preserving established production pipelines while injecting AI-driven acceleration. Editors retain full control over final cuts, approving, modifying, or rejecting AI-generated recommendations as appropriate.

## Outcomes for News Production Teams

The demonstrated approach delivers measurably faster story production while maintaining the editorial oversight essential to journalistic integrity. By automating the labor-intensive tasks of clip discovery and rough-cut assembly, news organizations free their creative talent to concentrate on narrative craft, fact-checking, and presentation quality. The combination of Amazon Bedrock, Amazon Nova, and partner-integrated workflows positions newsrooms to meet breaking-news demands without sacrificing the human expertise that defines credible reporting.
