# Graham Media's AI-Powered Newsroom Transformation

## Reimagining Local News Through Cloud-Native Architecture

Graham Media partnered with Amazon Web Services to fundamentally reshape how local news is produced, distributed, and consumed across its station portfolio. Faced with the dual pressures of evolving audience expectations and the operational realities of running multiple regional newsrooms, the company turned to cloud-native applications as the foundation for a comprehensive modernization initiative. Rather than incrementally upgrading legacy broadcast infrastructure, Graham Media chose to rebuild its core production workflows on AWS, creating an architecture designed for elasticity, geographic distribution, and continuous innovation.

The transformation centered on replacing siloed, location-bound systems with a unified cloud platform capable of supporting journalists, editors, and producers regardless of where they physically work. This shift unlocked the ability to operate newsrooms around the clock and across multiple markets without expanding headcount, allowing existing staff to collaborate seamlessly on shared content pipelines and respond to breaking news with greater speed and coordination.

## Embedding AI Across the Production Lifecycle

A defining element of the transformation is the integration of artificial intelligence directly into editorial and production workflows. Graham Media leverages AI to automate time-consuming tasks such as transcription, metadata generation, content tagging, and asset discovery, freeing journalists to focus on storytelling rather than manual processing. Intelligent content discovery capabilities allow staff to surface relevant archival footage, identify story connections, and repurpose material across platforms far more efficiently than traditional search methods permitted.

These AI-driven enhancements have measurably compressed production timelines, enabling faster turnaround from raw footage to published story. The result is a newsroom where machine learning augments human judgment at each stage, accelerating routine operations while preserving editorial control over the final product.

### Implementation Approach with AWS ProServe

Graham Media worked closely with AWS Professional Services to design and deploy the new architecture, drawing on ProServe's expertise to navigate the technical complexity of migrating broadcast-grade workflows to the cloud. This collaborative implementation model ensured that the platform was tailored to the specific demands of live local news while adhering to AWS best practices for scalability, security, and cost efficiency. The engagement produced not only a working system but also institutional knowledge that Graham Media's internal teams now use to extend and operate the platform independently.

### Business Outcomes and a Replicable Playbook

The business results validate the strategic bet on cloud and AI. Graham Media achieved 24/7 distributed operations, reduced production time, and improved content reuse, all without increasing staffing levels. Beyond the immediate operational gains, the project produced something equally valuable: a documented, repeatable playbook for newsroom modernization. This blueprint covers architecture patterns, implementation sequencing, and change management lessons, positioning Graham Media as a model for other media organizations seeking to undertake similar transformations and demonstrating how legacy broadcasters can compete effectively in a digital-first landscape.
