# Live Video Intelligence with TAMS and Agentic AI

## Transforming Live Event Coverage Through Real-Time Multimodal Analysis

Live event coverage produces moments of significant value that audiences expect to see almost instantly, whether those moments come from sports, breaking news, or large-scale entertainment broadcasts. Traditional production workflows, however, struggle to meet that expectation because they depend on hours of manual review, logging, and editing before any clip reaches distribution channels. This demonstration addresses that gap by showcasing how AWS infrastructure can enable real-time multimodal analysis of live video streams, dramatically shortening the path from event to audience. By treating the live feed as a continuously analyzable data source rather than a static asset awaiting post-production, broadcasters gain the ability to react to compelling moments the instant they occur.

## Combining TAMS with Parallel AI Agents

At the core of the architecture is the Time Addressable Media Store, known as TAMS, which provides a time-based abstraction over live and recorded media. TAMS allows any moment in the stream to be addressed precisely by timecode, making it possible for downstream systems to retrieve, reference, and assemble segments without copying or re-encoding the underlying media. Working alongside TAMS, multiple AI agents operate in parallel against the same live feed, each focused on a distinct task within the production pipeline. This agentic approach replaces the linear, human-driven workflow with a concurrent system in which several specialized models contribute their outputs simultaneously, allowing analysis, extraction, and assembly to happen in overlapping rather than sequential phases.

### Rolling Summaries, Clip Extraction, and Automated Highlight Reels

The agents continuously generate rolling summaries of the live broadcast, giving producers an always-current understanding of what has happened in the event so far without requiring anyone to watch the full feed. In parallel, extraction agents identify broadcast-quality clips around key moments, using multimodal cues from video, audio, and contextual signals to determine when something noteworthy has occurred. A separate assembly process then stitches selected moments into highlight reels as the event unfolds, so that completed packages are ready for distribution at or near the moment the action concludes. The combined effect is the compression of traditional multi-hour production cycles into a matter of minutes, enabling broadcasters to publish impactful clips and recap content while audience interest is still at its peak and giving production teams a scalable foundation for fast-turnaround live coverage.
