# Winning with Data: How AWS and Prime Video Are Redefining Sports Engagement

## Transforming the Fan Experience Through Cloud-Powered Analytics

The collaboration between Prime Video and AWS represents a significant shift in how sports content is produced, analyzed, and delivered to global audiences. By combining Prime Video's deep sports broadcasting expertise with AWS's cloud infrastructure and artificial intelligence capabilities, the partnership has unlocked new possibilities for broadcasters, leagues, and fans. The session frames this evolution as a direct response to growing audience demand for richer, more contextual sports storytelling, where data serves not as a backdrop but as a central narrative element that brings games to life.

At the heart of this transformation lies the ability to capture, process, and visualize massive volumes of real-time data during live events. Cloud-powered intelligence enables broadcasters to surface insights instantly, giving viewers a deeper understanding of strategy, performance, and momentum as it unfolds. This approach redefines what fans expect from a live broadcast, moving beyond traditional commentary into a fully data-augmented viewing experience.

## Innovations Across Major Sports Properties

### Prime Vision and Thursday Night Football

Thursday Night Football has served as a flagship showcase for what cloud-driven sports analytics can achieve. Prime Vision, the alternate broadcast experience, has evolved considerably since its debut, layering machine learning–driven graphics and contextual insights directly onto the live feed. Features such as defensive alerts, pressure probability, and player tracking visualizations transform how viewers interpret each play, illustrating how AI can translate raw tracking data into stories accessible to casual and expert fans alike.

### NFL IQ, NBA Analytics, and NASCAR Real-Time Data

Beyond Thursday Night Football, the partnership extends into NFL IQ, which reframes how fans understand draft strategy by exposing the analytical reasoning behind team decisions. In basketball, NBA statistical analysis powered by AWS reveals previously hidden dimensions of player and team performance, surfacing patterns that enrich both broadcast and fan engagement. NASCAR presents a different challenge altogether, where extreme speed and the volume of telemetry require real-time processing solutions that AWS infrastructure is uniquely positioned to deliver, ensuring insights reach viewers without latency.

### PGA Tour Shot-by-Shot Visualization

The PGA Tour partnership demonstrates how shot-by-shot data visualization can transform a traditionally slower-paced sport into a dynamic, analytically rich experience. By visualizing every shot's trajectory, distance, and strategic context, AWS helps the Tour deliver compelling storytelling that deepens fan understanding of player decisions and course management.

## Empowering Internal Teams with Quick Suite

A critical internal dimension of this transformation involves Quick Suite, which empowers Prime Video Sports teams to unlock deeper insights and accelerate decision-making behind the scenes. Rather than limiting analytics to on-air features, Quick Suite enables production, programming, and editorial teams to interrogate data quickly, identify trends, and make informed choices about coverage, storytelling angles, and content strategy. This internal acceleration ensures that the analytical sophistication visible to fans is matched by equally data-driven workflows within the organization.

## Outcomes and the Future of Sports Engagement

The session concludes by emphasizing that the convergence of Prime Video's sports expertise with AWS's AI and cloud capabilities creates unprecedented opportunities across the sports ecosystem. Broadcasters gain new tools for differentiation, leagues unlock fresh ways to engage audiences with their underlying data assets, and fans receive a viewing experience that is more immersive, informative, and personalized than ever before. The partnership signals a broader industry direction in which data-driven storytelling becomes inseparable from the live sports broadcast itself, setting new expectations for how games are watched and understood.
