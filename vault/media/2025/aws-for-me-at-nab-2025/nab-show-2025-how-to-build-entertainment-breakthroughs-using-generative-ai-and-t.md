# Building Entertainment Breakthroughs with Generative AI and the Cloud

## Industry Transformation in Media, Entertainment, Games, and Sports

The media and entertainment landscape is experiencing a fundamental transformation, driven by evolving viewer behaviors and an increasing demand for personalized, interactive experiences. In this NAB Show 2025 session, Samira Bakhtiar, General Manager of Media, Entertainment, Games and Sports at AWS, convenes a panel of industry leaders to explore how generative AI, cloud-based live production, and interactive technologies are reshaping content creation and audience engagement. The conversation features perspectives from Gérard Medioni of Prime Video, Ira Rubenstein of PBS, Devin Poolman of Cosm, and Raghvender Arni of AWS, each bringing distinct viewpoints from streaming, public broadcasting, immersive venues, and cloud innovation.

The discussion centers on a pivotal moment for the industry: audiences now expect content that is not only abundant across more platforms than ever, but also tailored, immersive, and accessible wherever they choose to engage. The panelists agree that the convergence of AI and cloud technologies provides the foundation for meeting these expectations at scale, enabling broadcasters, streamers, and creators alike to extend their reach and reimagine the viewer experience.

## Generative AI as a Catalyst for Content and Engagement

### Reinventing Content Creation and Personalization

Prime Video's Gérard Medioni outlines how generative AI is being applied to enrich the streaming experience, from automated content understanding to personalized recommendations and enhanced search. By leveraging machine learning models that analyze video, audio, and metadata, Prime Video is able to surface contextually relevant content and create new ways for viewers to discover and interact with programming. The emphasis is on using AI not as a replacement for creative work, but as an accelerator that frees teams to focus on storytelling while automating repetitive production tasks.

Ira Rubenstein of PBS discusses how public media is adopting AI to expand educational and cultural reach, particularly in making archival content discoverable and meaningful to new audiences. Generative AI helps PBS repurpose and contextualize its vast library, ensuring that legacy programming remains relevant in a streaming-first environment. This approach underscores a broader theme: AI enables organizations of all sizes and missions to do more with the content they already own.

### Immersive and Interactive Experiences

Devin Poolman of Cosm highlights how immersive venues are pushing the boundaries of fan engagement by combining live event broadcasts with shared, in-person experiences powered by advanced rendering and cloud production pipelines. Cosm's approach demonstrates how cloud infrastructure enables the delivery of high-fidelity, real-time content to specialized environments, blurring the lines between live attendance and remote viewing. The panel notes that interactive technologies, when paired with AI-driven content adaptation, allow audiences to participate in events rather than passively consume them.

## Cloud Infrastructure and the Path Forward

Raghvender Arni from AWS frames the technical foundation that makes these innovations possible, emphasizing live cloud production, scalable AI workloads, and connected workflows that span the entire content lifecycle. Cloud-based production removes traditional bottlenecks, enabling distributed teams to collaborate on live and on-demand content while integrating generative AI services directly into existing pipelines. The result is faster time-to-market, lower operational costs, and the flexibility to experiment with new formats.

The session concludes with a shared outlook: the combination of generative AI, cloud production, and interactive delivery is no longer experimental but essential. Panelists encourage broadcasters, streaming services, and creators to begin integrating these capabilities now, identifying high-impact use cases such as content discovery, archival monetization, personalized viewing, and immersive live events. The collective message is that organizations embracing this technological convergence will be best positioned to captivate audiences, unlock new revenue opportunities, and define the next era of entertainment.
