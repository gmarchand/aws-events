# Beyond Broadcast: AI, Data Analytics, and Personalization in Sports Media

## Overview of the Panel Discussion

At NAB 2025, Chris Blandy, Director of Strategic Business Development for Media & Entertainment, Games & Sports at AWS, moderated a forward-looking panel exploring how artificial intelligence, data analytics, and emerging technologies are reshaping the sports media landscape. The conversation brought together executives from Tagboard, Sportec Solutions AG, and FOX Sports, each representing a distinct vantage point on how broadcasters and rights holders are evolving beyond traditional linear delivery. The central premise of the discussion was that modern sports content must do more than transmit a live event — it must understand its audience, anticipate behavior, and respond in real time to deliver experiences tailored to individual fans.

## Harnessing AI and Data to Transform the Fan Experience

### Unlocking Audience Insights and Predicting Viewership

A major thread of the panel focused on how AI and advanced analytics are enabling media companies to develop a far deeper understanding of their audiences. The panelists discussed how machine learning models, trained on viewership patterns, engagement signals, and contextual sports data, allow broadcasters to predict which moments will resonate, which matchups will draw the largest audiences, and how content strategy should adapt across platforms. Sportec Solutions AG highlighted the role of granular sports data — including player tracking and event metadata — in feeding these predictive systems, while FOX Sports emphasized how these insights inform editorial and programming decisions that drive measurable engagement gains.

### Creating Personalized and Interactive Experiences

The panelists also explored how personalization has become a strategic imperative rather than a feature. Tagboard discussed how social and second-screen integrations help broadcasters surface relevant moments and community-driven content directly within the viewing experience, deepening fan connection. FOX Sports shared its approach to tailoring storylines, statistics, and supplementary content to individual viewer preferences, leveraging cloud-scale infrastructure on AWS to deliver these experiences reliably during peak live events. The group agreed that combining real-time data, generative AI, and flexible cloud architecture is what makes large-scale personalization commercially viable today.

### The Road Ahead for Sports Media

Closing the discussion, the panelists looked toward the future of sports entertainment, where AI-driven workflows, automated content production, and immersive interactive formats will continue to converge. They underscored that collaboration between rights holders, technology providers, and cloud platforms like AWS will be essential to scaling these innovations responsibly. The shared outcome of the conversation was a clear conviction that the next era of sports media will be defined not by the broadcast itself, but by the intelligence, personalization, and interactivity layered around it.
