# Closer to the Action: How IMAX and AWS Are Enhancing Live Sports Streaming

## Setting the Stage at NAB 2025

During the Sports Summit at NAB 2025, Chris Blandy, Director of Strategic Business Development for Media & Entertainment, Games, and Sports at AWS, hosted a conversation with two senior leaders from IMAX Corporation: Abdul Rehman, Chief Product Officer, and Vikram Arumilli, General Manager of Streaming and Consumer Technology and Senior Vice President. Together, they examined how the partnership between IMAX and AWS is reshaping the way live sports reach audiences, both inside theaters and in living rooms. The discussion centered on a shared ambition: bringing fans closer to the action by delivering premium, cinema-grade quality at the scale that modern streaming demands.

## Elevating the Live Sports Experience

### Translating Cinematic Quality to the Home

Rehman and Arumilli emphasized that IMAX has long defined what immersive viewing means in theaters, and the company is now extending that expertise into streaming. The core challenge, they explained, lies in preserving the visual fidelity, sound depth, and emotional impact of a premium venue when content travels over the open internet to millions of homes simultaneously. By applying IMAX's image enhancement and remastering technologies — refined over decades for the largest screens in the world — to live sports feeds, the company aims to give remote viewers a sense of presence that conventional broadcasts cannot match. This means sharper detail during fast-paced action, more accurate color representation, and audio that conveys the atmosphere of the venue itself.

### Cloud Infrastructure as the Enabler

The panel made clear that none of this would be possible without the elasticity and global reach of AWS. Live sports streaming requires the ability to scale instantly to massive concurrent audiences, process video in real time with minimal latency, and distribute it reliably across geographies. Blandy and the IMAX leaders discussed how AWS provides the compute, networking, and media services that allow IMAX's enhancement pipelines to run in the cloud rather than in fixed facilities. This shift transforms what was once a bespoke, venue-bound capability into a scalable service that can be applied to any live event, anywhere in the world.

## Scalability, Partnership, and the Path Forward

### Making Premium Experiences Repeatable

A recurring theme was the difference between what is technically possible for a single marquee event and what is operationally scalable across an entire sports calendar. The IMAX executives stressed that their collaboration with AWS is specifically designed to make premium live experiences repeatable and economical, so that leagues, broadcasters, and rights holders can offer cinema-quality streams as a regular product rather than as a one-off showcase. Combining IMAX's proprietary processing with AWS's managed media workflows shortens the path from live capture to enhanced delivery, reducing the engineering lift required from each new partner.

### Outcomes and the Road Ahead

The conversation concluded with a forward-looking view in which the boundary between theatrical and at-home sports viewing continues to blur. As cloud-based enhancement matures, fans should expect streaming experiences that rival being in the stadium or in an IMAX auditorium, with consistent quality across devices and audiences of any size. The collective takeaway from Blandy, Rehman, and Arumilli was that the combination of IMAX's quality standards and AWS's infrastructure represents a practical blueprint for the next generation of live sports — one where immersion is no longer reserved for the few who can attend in person but becomes the default expectation for every viewer.
