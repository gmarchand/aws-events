# AI FrameLift: Archive Enhancement for Editors

## Unlocking the Value of Historical Media Libraries

Media companies hold vast archives of historical footage that often go underutilized because the original capture resolution falls short of what modern audiences expect on contemporary displays. Traditionally, bringing this content up to current standards has required costly bulk upscaling projects, which consume significant time and resources while processing material that may never actually be used in production. AI FrameLift addresses this challenge by offering a targeted, on-demand approach to resolution enhancement, allowing editorial teams to extract maximum value from legacy content without the overhead of processing entire libraries.

## A Targeted, Editor-Friendly Workflow

### Super Resolution On Demand from the Editing Environment

Rather than upscaling everything in advance, AI FrameLift empowers editors to apply super resolution selectively to the specific clips or even individual frames they need, exactly when they need them. The capability integrates directly into the familiar editing environment, eliminating the need to switch tools or interrupt creative momentum. Editors can elevate low-resolution archival content to resolutions of up to 2K on a per-segment basis, ensuring that enhancement effort is focused only on the material destined for the final production.

### Flexible Deployment with GPU-Accelerated AI

The solution runs equally well on-premises and in the cloud, giving teams the flexibility to align with their existing infrastructure strategy. It leverages AWS GPU-accelerated instances to execute the AI models responsible for upscaling, delivering the computational performance required to enhance footage rapidly without bogging down local workstations. This architecture allows production teams to scale processing power up or down based on project demands while maintaining a consistent editorial experience.

## Faster, More Economical Production Outcomes

By replacing bulk upscaling with a precise, frame-aware approach, AI FrameLift compresses production timelines and reduces overall costs. Editors avoid waiting on large batch jobs, archives remain accessible without lengthy preprocessing, and budget is preserved for the segments that truly contribute to the final piece. The result is a workflow that lets media organizations bring compelling historical content to modern audiences faster, more economically, and without compromising the creative process.
