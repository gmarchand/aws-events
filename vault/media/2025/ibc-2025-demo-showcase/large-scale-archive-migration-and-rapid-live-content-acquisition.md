# Large-Scale Archive Migration and Rapid Live Content Acquisition

## Overview of the Media Storage Challenge

Media organizations today face a complex set of challenges that limit their ability to extract full value from their content libraries. Traditional on-premises storage systems generate substantial costs, create isolated content silos, and prevent organizations from harnessing modern cloud-native capabilities. These legacy environments make it difficult to apply AI-driven workflows that could otherwise transform vast archives into strategic business assets, leaving valuable content underutilized and innovation constrained.

This demonstration addresses these pain points by showing how AWS services, combined with partner solutions from Cloudfirst.io and Dalet, enable media organizations to transition their entire content ecosystem to the cloud. The result is a unified, modernized environment in which archives become discoverable assets and live content flows seamlessly into intelligent workflows.

## Migrating Archives and Modernizing Asset Management

### Seamless Archive Migration with Cloudfirst.io

The first stage of the modernization journey focuses on moving large-scale archives from on-premises systems into AWS. Cloudfirst.io delivers the migration tooling that allows organizations to transfer massive volumes of historical content efficiently, while Dalet provides the asset management layer that ensures migrated content remains accessible and well-organized. Together, these partner solutions break down content silos and replace fragmented storage approaches with a coherent cloud-based foundation.

### Intelligent Indexing Through the Media Lake on AWS

Once content arrives in AWS, the Guidance for a Media Lake on AWS orchestrates automated indexing of the migrated archives. This orchestration applies AI services to generate rich metadata, enabling semantic search across the entire archive. Editors, producers, and other users can therefore discover relevant material using natural, meaning-based queries rather than relying on rigid keyword matching or manual cataloging. This transformation turns dormant archives into active, searchable resources that fuel new production workflows.

## Live Content Acquisition and Strategic Outcomes

### Time-Addressable Media Store Workflows for Live Content

In parallel with archive modernization, the demonstration shows how live content is ingested using Time-Addressable Media Store (TAMS) workflows. TAMS provides a modern approach to handling live media in the cloud, allowing rapid acquisition and downstream processing of newly created content. By unifying live ingest with archive access in the same cloud environment, organizations can apply consistent AI-driven enrichment and discovery across both historical and current material.

### Business Value and Cost Benefits

The strategic case for migration extends well beyond technical modernization. Organizations that adopt this architecture reduce infrastructure overhead, eliminate the constraints of legacy storage, and gain access to AI-powered content discovery that increases the value of every asset. The combined approach demonstrated through AWS, Cloudfirst.io, and Dalet positions media enterprises to operate more efficiently, innovate faster, and turn their content libraries into long-term strategic assets that support new business opportunities.
