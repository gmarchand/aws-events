# Transforming Supply Chains with Amazon Bedrock AgentCore

## Overview of the Session

This AWS re:Invent 2025 session, presented by Fujitsu as an AWS Partner, examines how modern supply chain management (SCM) systems must evolve to address the rapidly shifting requirements, disruptions, and limitations that have defined recent years. The presentation centers on the application of agentic and autonomous AI agents — built on Amazon Bedrock AgentCore — to reimagine how supply chain teams confront growing operational complexity and help customers adapt to increasingly dynamic markets.

## The Supply Chain Challenge and the Case for Agentic AI

### Why Traditional SCM Falls Short

Supply chain management has grown markedly more difficult, with volatility in demand, supply availability, logistics, and regulatory conditions placing pressure on legacy systems. Traditional rule-based platforms struggle to react quickly enough to these constantly changing variables, leaving planners and operators to absorb complexity manually. The session frames this gap as the core motivation for rethinking SCM with autonomous, reasoning-capable AI rather than incremental automation.

### Applying Agentic and Autonomous AI

Fujitsu's approach moves beyond static workflows by deploying AI agents that can perceive context, reason about trade-offs, and take action across supply chain processes. These agents are designed to operate semi-autonomously alongside human teams, accelerating decision-making in areas where speed and adaptability are now competitive differentiators. The emphasis is on reducing the cognitive load on supply chain professionals while improving responsiveness to disruption.

## Building on Amazon Bedrock AgentCore

### Leveraging AgentCore Capabilities

Amazon Bedrock AgentCore provides the foundational platform for Fujitsu's solution, offering the runtime, orchestration, and integration primitives needed to deploy production-grade agents. By building on AgentCore, Fujitsu accelerates development of agents that can connect to enterprise data sources, invoke supply chain tools, and coordinate multi-step reasoning without requiring teams to assemble these capabilities from scratch.

### Outcomes for Customers

The resulting architecture allows supply chain teams to address complexity that previously required extensive manual analysis, helping customers adapt faster to market shifts. The session positions this combination of Fujitsu's domain expertise and AWS's agent infrastructure as a path for enterprises to modernize SCM operations, improve agility, and unlock new efficiencies through autonomous AI rather than incremental tooling upgrades.
