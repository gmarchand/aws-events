# Next-Gen Media Production: Amazon Nova Reinventing Bundesliga Content

## Reimagining Sports Media Through Generative AI

The Bundesliga, Germany's premier soccer league, has embarked on an ambitious transformation of its media production pipeline by partnering with AWS to leverage Amazon Nova foundation models. This AWS re:Invent 2025 session highlights how one of the world's most-watched sports leagues is using generative AI to scale content creation, deepen fan engagement, and reach a global audience that increasingly demands personalized, localized experiences. The central thesis presented is that traditional broadcast workflows alone cannot satisfy the expectations of a connected, multilingual fan base, and that generative AI now offers a path to producing more content, in more languages, with greater relevance than ever before.

## Scaling Global Reach With Amazon Nova

### Multilingual Content Creation and Translation

A core focus of the session is how the Bundesliga uses Amazon Nova to generate and translate media content at scale, enabling the league to engage its "next billion fans" in their native languages. Rather than relying solely on regional broadcast partners or manual localization, the league employs Nova's generative capabilities to produce match recaps, narratives, and commentary that can be rapidly adapted across linguistic and cultural contexts. This approach dramatically lowers the cost and turnaround time of localization while maintaining editorial quality, allowing the Bundesliga to publish content in markets where dedicated production teams would otherwise be uneconomical.

### Searching the World's Largest Soccer Archive

The session also showcases how AWS-powered AI unlocks the value of the Bundesliga's enormous historical footage library. By applying multimodal models to index plays, players, moments, and statistics, the league has made decades of archival material searchable through natural-language queries. Producers, journalists, and ultimately fans can retrieve specific moments — a particular goal, a tactical sequence, a player milestone — in seconds, transforming what was once a labor-intensive archival task into an on-demand creative resource.

### A Generative AI Fan Companion

Building on these foundations, the Bundesliga has developed a generative AI companion designed to democratize access to personalized insights. Fans can ask conversational questions about matches, players, and statistics, and receive tailored answers drawn from the league's data and footage. This companion turns passive viewers into active explorers of the sport, offering everyone — not just analysts and journalists — the kind of deep statistical and visual access previously reserved for professionals.

## Outcomes and Strategic Implications

### Enhanced Fan Experience and Operational Efficiency

The combined effect of these initiatives is a richer, more participatory fan experience and a more efficient content operation. Personalization, archive search, and automated multilingual production reinforce one another: the same AI infrastructure that powers fan-facing tools also accelerates internal media workflows for broadcast partners. This dual benefit positions the Bundesliga to grow internationally without proportionally scaling its production headcount.

### A Blueprint for Sports and Media Organizations

The session ultimately presents the Bundesliga's work as a blueprint for other sports leagues and media rights holders. The decision to standardize on Amazon Nova reflects a strategic bet that foundation models — coupled with rich proprietary data and footage — can become a durable competitive advantage in fan engagement. Attendees leave with a clear view of how generative AI can be operationalized in live sports media, from translation and content generation to intelligent archive access and conversational fan products.
