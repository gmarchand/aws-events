# NBA Inside The Game, Powered by AWS: Building the NBA's New Stats Program

## Overview of the Partnership

The NBA has launched a new advanced statistics program called "Inside The Game, Powered by AWS," designed to deepen fan engagement with basketball by surfacing insights that go far beyond the traditional box score. During every NBA game, billions of data points are captured in real time, ranging from each player's shooting performance to skeletal pose tracking that records body position on the court sixty times per second. This volume and granularity of data forms the foundation of a sophisticated analytics platform that powers a new generation of stats developed through artificial intelligence and machine learning. The session presented at AWS re:Invent 2025 detailed how the NBA collaborated with AWS to migrate its advanced stats infrastructure and to engineer entirely new metrics that capture aspects of the game previously invisible to viewers.

## Migrating the Advanced Stats Platform to AWS

A central decision discussed in the session was the NBA's migration of its advanced stats platform from Azure to AWS. This move was driven by the need for a more scalable and integrated environment capable of handling the enormous data volumes generated during live games while supporting increasingly complex AI and ML workloads. By consolidating tracking data, video feeds, and derived analytics on AWS, the NBA gained the ability to develop, train, and deploy machine learning models more efficiently and to deliver insights to fans, broadcasters, and teams with greater speed and reliability. The migration also positioned the league to expand its suite of AI-driven stats without being constrained by the limitations of its previous infrastructure.

### Architecting for Real-Time Intelligence

The new platform is built to ingest and process player tracking data continuously throughout each possession, applying predictive models on the fly. This architecture allows the league to combine historical advanced stats with live data streams, generating contextual insights that update as plays unfold. The result is an analytics environment that supports both deep retrospective analysis and real-time storytelling during broadcasts.

## Building the Gravity Stat

The headline outcome of the AWS partnership is a new metric called Gravity, which quantifies how strongly an offensive player pulls defenders toward them during every possession. Rather than relying on simple proximity measures, Gravity leverages existing NBA advanced stats together with a predictive machine learning model that evaluates defensive attention in real time. The model interprets the spatial relationships between offensive and defensive players, factoring in player tendencies and situational context to estimate the gravitational pull each athlete exerts at any given moment.

### Why Gravity Matters for Fans and Teams

Gravity captures a dimension of basketball that has long been understood intuitively by coaches and analysts but rarely measured with precision. Elite scorers and shooters distort defensive shapes simply by being on the court, creating opportunities for teammates that never appear in traditional stat lines. By translating this influence into a concrete, real-time number, the NBA gives fans a new lens through which to appreciate off-ball impact and team dynamics, while providing teams and broadcasters with a richer vocabulary for describing the game.

## Outcomes and Future Direction

The session concluded by framing Inside The Game as the foundation for an expanding portfolio of AI-driven stats that will continue to evolve as the platform matures on AWS. The successful migration, combined with the launch of Gravity, demonstrates how cloud infrastructure, machine learning, and sports data can converge to transform fan experiences. The NBA and AWS signaled their intent to continue building advanced metrics that reveal previously hidden aspects of basketball, reinforcing a long-term commitment to using cloud-native AI to bring fans closer to the game.
