# Condé Nast's Digital Reinvention on AWS

## Transforming a Legacy Media Powerhouse

At AWS re:Invent 2025, Sanjay Bhakta presented Condé Nast's sweeping digital transformation, detailing how the global media company reinvented itself by migrating more than 800 properties to AWS infrastructure. The initiative represents one of the most ambitious cloud migrations in the publishing industry, encompassing iconic brands and titles that collectively reach hundreds of millions of readers worldwide. Bhakta framed the effort not merely as a technology upgrade, but as a fundamental rethinking of how a heritage media organization operates in the cloud-native era.

## From Data-Rich to Insight-Driven

The core motivation behind the transformation was Condé Nast's recognition that, despite generating enormous volumes of content and audience engagement data, the organization had historically been data-rich but insights-poor. Disparate systems, siloed brand operations, and aging infrastructure prevented the company from extracting meaningful intelligence from its assets. By consolidating its digital estate on AWS, Condé Nast established a unified foundation capable of supporting modern analytics, machine learning, and personalization at scale. This shift enabled editors, marketers, and product teams to access consistent, timely insights across the entire portfolio rather than working from fragmented brand-level views.

### A Partner Ecosystem Approach

Rather than building the new platform in isolation, Condé Nast assembled a strategic partner ecosystem anchored by AWS and complemented by Databricks for advanced analytics and lakehouse capabilities, along with Snowplow for behavioral data collection and event tracking. This combination allowed the company to capture granular reader interactions, process them through scalable data pipelines, and feed the resulting intelligence back into editorial and product decisions. The partnership model accelerated time to value while ensuring that each component of the stack was optimized by specialists in its domain.

### Personalized Content Delivery at Scale

A central outcome of the migration is the company's new capacity to deliver personalized content experiences across its hundreds of properties. By unifying audience data and applying machine learning on AWS, Condé Nast can tailor recommendations, surface relevant stories, and adapt experiences to individual reader preferences in ways that were previously impossible within its legacy environment. Bhakta emphasized that this personalization capability is critical for sustaining engagement and subscription growth in a competitive digital media landscape, positioning Condé Nast to compete effectively with native digital platforms while preserving the editorial standards of its heritage brands.

## Outcomes and Strategic Direction

The transformation has repositioned Condé Nast as a cloud-native media organization with the agility to launch new experiences, integrate emerging AI capabilities, and operate efficiently across a global portfolio. Bhakta concluded that the migration is not an endpoint but a foundation, enabling continued innovation in content delivery, audience intelligence, and monetization as the company evolves alongside its readers and the broader media industry.
