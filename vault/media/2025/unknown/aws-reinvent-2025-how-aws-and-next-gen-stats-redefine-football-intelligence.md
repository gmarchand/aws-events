# How AWS and Next Gen Stats Redefine Football Intelligence

## A Decade of Transforming Football Through Data and Machine Learning

In 2015, the NFL and AWS forged a partnership that would fundamentally change how fans, broadcasters, coaches, and players experience professional football. This session at AWS re:Invent 2025 commemorates ten years of Next Gen Stats, charting the journey from rudimentary sport metrics to a sophisticated machine learning ecosystem that now operates at a scale few sports organizations have ever attempted. The narrative unfolds across three temporal lenses—Then, Now, and Next—each illustrating how cloud infrastructure and advanced analytics have progressively unlocked deeper layers of football intelligence.

## From Basic Metrics to Sophisticated Intelligence

### The Origins: Giving Fans a New Vocabulary

When Next Gen Stats first launched, its purpose was straightforward yet transformative. By outfitting players and equipment with sensors and ingesting positional data into the AWS cloud, the NFL began surfacing metrics that fans had never seen before—speed, separation, and distance traveled. These early statistics provided audiences with a richer language for discussing the game, turning intuition about athletic performance into quantifiable facts. This foundational period established the data pipelines, ingestion patterns, and partnership culture that would later support far more ambitious capabilities.

### The Present: Probability Engines and Hidden Dimensions

The current era of Next Gen Stats reflects a dramatic leap in analytical sophistication. AWS now powers a suite of machine learning models that quantify aspects of football once considered too nuanced to measure. Completion probability evaluates the likelihood of a pass succeeding given the spatial relationships of every player on the field. Expected yards predicts ball-carrier outcomes based on blocking schemes and defender positioning. Pressure prediction anticipates pass-rush effectiveness in real time, and coverage responsibility assigns defensive accountability to individual players on every snap. These probability engines process billions of data points to deliver insights during live broadcasts, inform player safety initiatives, and even underpin innovative fan experiences such as Toy Story Funday Football, where real-time tracking data is mapped onto animated characters in a fully rendered virtual world.

### The Future: An AI-First Football Architecture

Looking ahead, AWS is architecting the next chapter of football intelligence around predictive and generative AI capabilities operating at NFL scale. The vision extends beyond enhanced statistics into immersive, personalized, and conversational experiences powered by foundation models. This forward-looking roadmap positions generative AI as a means of synthesizing complex game situations, producing tailored narratives for diverse audiences, and enabling new forms of interaction between fans and the sport. The session frames this evolution not merely as a sports analytics achievement but as a broader masterclass in operating machine learning and artificial intelligence systems at unprecedented scale.

## Outcomes and Broader Significance

The ten-year collaboration between the NFL and AWS demonstrates how sustained investment in cloud infrastructure, real-time data processing, and machine learning research can reshape an entire industry. The decisions made along this journey—standardizing on AWS for ingestion and inference, prioritizing probability-based models over deterministic statistics, and extending insights from broadcast enhancement into player safety and entertainment innovation—offer a template for organizations seeking to operationalize AI at scale. The session ultimately underscores that the technological foundations powering Next Gen Stats are transferable lessons in handling massive data volumes, deploying low-latency ML inference, and continually evolving an analytics platform to meet rising expectations from fans, partners, and the league itself.
