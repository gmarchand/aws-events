# Migrating a Leading Global Sports League to AWS

## Overview of the NBA and AWS Partnership

The NBA and AWS officially tipped off their strategic partnership at the start of the 2025-26 season, marking a pivotal moment in how one of the world's most prominent sports leagues delivers content and experiences to its global fan base. This session provided an exclusive look into the early stages of the NBA's cloud transformation journey, detailing how the league approached large-scale migrations across multiple business-critical workloads. The collaboration represents more than a technology shift; it reflects a deliberate decision by the NBA to modernize its digital foundation in order to keep pace with the growing expectations of millions of basketball fans worldwide.

## Strategic Approach to Migration

### Choosing AWS and Defining the Migration Strategy

The NBA's decision to partner with AWS was driven by the need for scale, reliability, and the ability to innovate quickly across a wide range of business functions. Rather than approaching the migration as a single monolithic project, the league developed a comprehensive strategy that segmented workloads by business function, allowing teams to prioritize systems based on impact and complexity. Key workloads moved to AWS included the advanced stats platform that powers analytics across the league, the media workflows that drive content production and distribution, and the broader IT infrastructure that supports day-to-day operations. By organizing the migration along these functional lines, the NBA was able to manage risk while delivering measurable improvements throughout the journey.

### Modernizing the Direct-to-Consumer Platform

A central focus of the session was the migration and modernization of the NBA's Direct-to-Consumer platform, which delivers live games, on-demand content, and interactive experiences to fans around the world. The architecture was rebuilt as a scalable, event-driven system designed to absorb the dramatic traffic spikes that accompany live game events and to process real-time data streams without compromising the viewing experience. The platform leverages Kubernetes clusters to orchestrate containerized workloads, non-relational databases to support flexible and high-throughput data access patterns, and AWS media services to handle the demanding requirements of live streaming and content delivery at global scale.

## Outcomes and Lessons Learned

### Building for Peak Traffic and Real-Time Engagement

The modernized architecture positions the NBA to deliver consistent, high-quality experiences during the most demanding moments of the season, when millions of fans connect simultaneously to watch live games and interact with real-time statistics. The combination of event-driven design, container orchestration, and purpose-built AWS services gives the league the elasticity it needs to scale up instantly during peak events and scale down efficiently when demand subsides. This approach not only improves performance but also creates a foundation for future innovation, allowing the NBA to introduce new fan experiences and data-driven features more rapidly than was previously possible. The session ultimately illustrated how a thoughtful, phased migration strategy combined with cloud-native modernization can transform a global sports league into a more agile and fan-centric digital organization.
