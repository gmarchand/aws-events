# AWS Public Sector Symposium Ottawa 2025 — Keynote Summary

## Driving Public Sector Transformation in Canada

The keynote at the AWS Public Sector Symposium Ottawa 2025 centered on how Amazon Web Services empowers Canadian public sector organizations to modernize the services they deliver to citizens while addressing the increasingly complex demands of security, digital sovereignty, and sustainability. Dave Levy, AWS Vice President of Worldwide Public Sector, framed the discussion around the accelerating pace of cloud adoption across government, education, and nonprofit organizations, emphasizing that public institutions worldwide are leveraging cloud technology not only to reduce costs but to fundamentally reimagine how they serve constituents. Kirsten Robinson, Federal Leader for AWS Canada, reinforced this Canadian focus by highlighting how AWS continues to invest locally in infrastructure, talent, and partnerships that support federal priorities and meet the unique regulatory expectations of Canadian institutions.

### Sovereignty, Security, and Sustainable Infrastructure

A major theme of the keynote was the intersection of sovereignty and security in modern cloud architecture. Jesse Dougherty, AWS Vice President of Global Networking, explored how AWS designs its global network and infrastructure to give customers granular control over where data resides, how it moves, and who can access it. He explained that the underlying networking fabric of AWS is engineered to deliver both performance and resilience while supporting the strict data residency and compliance frameworks that Canadian public sector organizations require. Sustainability was woven throughout this discussion, with the keynote underscoring that responsible infrastructure design — including efficient hardware, renewable energy commitments, and optimized networking — is essential to meeting public sector environmental obligations alongside operational ones.

## Modernizing Government Services Through Cloud

John Ostrander, Business Lead for Benefits Delivery Modernization and IT at Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC), shared a practical perspective on how a major federal department is transforming the way Canadians receive critical benefits. He described the Benefits Delivery Modernization program as a multi-year effort to replace aging legacy systems with modern, cloud-based platforms capable of delivering more responsive, reliable, and personalized services to millions of Canadians who depend on programs like Employment Insurance, Old Age Security, and the Canada Pension Plan. Ostrander emphasized that the move to cloud is not simply a technology refresh but a deliberate strategy to improve citizen experience, increase resilience, and provide the agility needed to adapt programs as policy and demographics evolve.

### Lessons from a Large-Scale Government Modernization

Ostrander's discussion offered insights into how ESDC is managing the complexity of migrating mission-critical workloads while maintaining uninterrupted service delivery. He stressed the importance of strong governance, close collaboration with cloud providers, and a workforce strategy that builds internal cloud expertise. The narrative reinforced that successful public sector modernization depends on aligning technology decisions with clear citizen-outcome goals, and on choosing partners that understand the regulatory, security, and accessibility expectations that come with serving the Canadian public.

## Advancing AI Innovation with Canadian Leadership

The keynote also turned to artificial intelligence, with Joelle Pineau, Chief AI Officer at Cohere, joining the discussion to explore the role of generative AI in the public sector. Pineau spoke about how Cohere — a leading Canadian AI company — is building enterprise-grade large language models that prioritize accuracy, security, and data privacy, qualities that are essential for government adoption. She highlighted the importance of sovereign AI capabilities for Canada and explained how partnerships between AWS and Cohere give public sector customers access to advanced AI tools while maintaining the controls necessary to meet national requirements.

### Responsible AI and the Path Forward

Pineau emphasized that responsible AI deployment in government depends on transparency, rigorous evaluation, and human oversight. She encouraged public sector leaders to begin experimenting with high-value, lower-risk use cases — such as document summarization, citizen-facing assistance, and internal productivity tools — while building the governance structures needed to scale AI responsibly. The keynote closed with a unified message from Levy and the guests: Canada's public sector is positioned to lead in delivering modern, secure, and AI-enabled services, and AWS is committed to supporting that journey through continued investment in infrastructure, partnerships, and the people who serve Canadians every day.
