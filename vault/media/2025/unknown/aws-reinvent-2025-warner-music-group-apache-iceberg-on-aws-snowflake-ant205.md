# Warner Music Group: Apache Iceberg on AWS and Snowflake

## Session Overview

At AWS re:Invent 2025, Michael Jones, Director of Software Engineering at Warner Music Group (WMG), delivered session ANT205 to share how one of the world's largest music companies is modernizing its data infrastructure using Apache Iceberg in combination with AWS and Snowflake. Presented in partnership with Snowflake, an AWS Partner, the session focused on the strategic role that open table formats play in supporting analytics, machine learning, and AI initiatives across the music and entertainment industry.

## Building an Interoperable Data Ecosystem

### The Business Context for Open Data Formats

Warner Music Group operates in an industry where data drives nearly every decision, from artist development and royalty management to streaming analytics and audience engagement. Jones framed the discussion around the need to unify a sprawling landscape of first-party data, generated internally across labels and publishing operations, with third-party data flowing in from streaming platforms, social media, and licensing partners. Traditional approaches that lock data into proprietary formats limited WMG's ability to act quickly on this information, prompting the company to adopt an open, interoperable architecture.

### Why Apache Iceberg

Apache Iceberg emerged as the foundation of WMG's modern data strategy because it allows the company to store data once and query it from multiple engines without duplication or vendor lock-in. Jones explained how Iceberg's table format delivers transactional guarantees, schema evolution, and time travel capabilities that are essential for managing large, frequently changing datasets. By standardizing on Iceberg, Warner Music can maintain a single source of truth on AWS storage while exposing that data consistently to analytics workloads, data science teams, and partner integrations.

### Pairing Iceberg with Snowflake on AWS

A central theme of the session was how WMG combines Iceberg's openness with Snowflake's performance and ease of use. Snowflake's native support for Iceberg tables allows Warner Music's analysts to query data stored in AWS using familiar SQL interfaces while preserving the flexibility to use other engines on the same underlying tables. Jones described how this pairing eliminates costly data movement, simplifies governance, and accelerates time to insight. The architecture leverages AWS services for storage and compute foundations while relying on Snowflake to deliver fast, governed analytics on top of the open format.

## Outcomes and Forward-Looking Initiatives

### Unlocking Deeper Analytics Today

The immediate payoff for Warner Music has been richer, more timely analytics across business domains. By unifying first- and third-party sources within Iceberg tables, teams gain a more complete view of artist performance, audience behavior, and revenue streams. Jones emphasized that this unified view reduces the friction historically associated with reconciling disparate datasets and enables business stakeholders to make decisions with greater confidence.

### Setting the Stage for Machine Learning and AI

Looking ahead, WMG is positioning its Iceberg-based foundation as the launchpad for advanced machine learning and generative AI initiatives on AWS. Because the data is open, well-governed, and accessible to multiple compute engines, data science teams can experiment with new models and AI services without rebuilding pipelines or duplicating storage. Jones closed by reinforcing that the combination of Apache Iceberg, AWS, and Snowflake gives Warner Music the agility to innovate at the pace the entertainment industry demands while protecting long-term flexibility through open standards.
