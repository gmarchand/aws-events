# Engineering at Full Throttle: How AWS Powers Formula 1

## Overview of the AWS and Formula 1 Partnership

At AWS re:Invent 2025, Formula 1 analyst and AWS Motorsports Ambassador Ruth Buscombe delivered a compelling session exploring the strategic partnership between Amazon Web Services and Formula 1. The session illuminated how cloud technology has fundamentally transformed one of the world's most data-intensive sports, where milliseconds determine victory and engineering precision defines competitive advantage. Buscombe drew on her deep expertise in motorsport strategy to demonstrate how AWS serves as the technological backbone enabling Formula 1 to operate at the cutting edge of innovation, both on the racetrack and across its global fan ecosystem.

## Transforming the Sport Through Cloud Innovation

### Powering Performance On the Track

The collaboration between AWS and Formula 1 centers on harnessing the enormous volume of telemetry and sensor data generated during every race weekend. Each Formula 1 car produces vast streams of real-time data covering tire performance, aerodynamic behavior, engine dynamics, and driver inputs, all of which must be processed and analyzed at extraordinary speeds. AWS provides the compute power, machine learning capabilities, and scalable infrastructure required to turn this raw data into actionable insights. Buscombe explained how engineers and strategists rely on cloud-based analytics to make split-second decisions, optimize race strategy, and push the boundaries of car development through computational fluid dynamics simulations performed in the cloud rather than in costly physical wind tunnels.

### Enhancing the Fan Experience Through F1 Insights and Track Pulse

A significant portion of the session focused on the fan-facing innovations made possible through AWS technology. F1 Insights, powered by AWS, delivers advanced statistics and predictive analytics directly to broadcasts and digital platforms, transforming how viewers understand the complexity of racing. Metrics such as predicted pit stop strategies, car performance scores, battle forecasts, and overtaking probabilities give fans unprecedented visibility into the strategic chess match unfolding at over 300 kilometers per hour. Track Pulse extends this engagement by providing immersive, data-driven storytelling that connects audiences to the emotional and technical drama of each race, deepening the connection between the sport and its global community.

### Driving the Future of Motorsport

Buscombe emphasized that the partnership extends beyond current applications, pointing toward an evolving future where artificial intelligence and machine learning will play increasingly central roles in race strategy, car design, and fan personalization. The discussion highlighted how AWS enables Formula 1 to innovate continuously, supporting initiatives ranging from sustainability efforts and reduced carbon footprint in research and development to the creation of new generations of more competitive and exciting racing through data-informed regulatory changes.

## Key Takeaways and Closing Reflections

The session reinforced that Formula 1 represents one of the most demanding proving grounds for cloud technology, where reliability, low latency, and computational scale are non-negotiable requirements. The partnership demonstrates how AWS services translate into tangible competitive and commercial outcomes, accelerating engineering cycles, enriching broadcasts, and expanding the sport's reach to new audiences worldwide. Attendees left with a clearer understanding of how cloud computing underpins modern motorsport and were treated to a replay of the recent Formula 1 Las Vegas Grand Prix, reinforcing the spectacle that this technological partnership helps deliver.
