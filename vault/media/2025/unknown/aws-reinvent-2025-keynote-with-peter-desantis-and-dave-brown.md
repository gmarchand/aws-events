# AWS re:Invent 2025 Keynote: Peter DeSantis and Dave Brown on Compute Innovation

## Overview of the Keynote

At AWS re:Invent 2025, Peter DeSantis, Senior Vice President of AWS, joined Dave Brown, Vice President of Compute and Machine Learning Services, to deliver a deep technical exploration of the foundational technologies powering AWS. The keynote emphasized AWS's distinctive culture of vertical innovation, where engineering teams work across the full stack — from custom silicon design through hypervisors, networking, and managed services — to deliver performance, efficiency, and security advantages that would be difficult to achieve through off-the-shelf components alone. The session reinforced AWS's long-standing strategy of investing in proprietary hardware and software to give customers measurable improvements in price-performance.

## Silicon-Level Innovation and the Graviton Journey

### The Evolution of Custom Compute

A central theme of the keynote was AWS's continued investment in custom silicon as a competitive differentiator. DeSantis and Brown traced the trajectory of the Graviton processor family, highlighting how each generation has expanded the performance envelope while reducing energy consumption and total cost of ownership for customers. They emphasized that this iterative approach — refining the chip, the server design, and the broader Nitro system in parallel — has allowed AWS to deliver compounding gains rather than isolated improvements.

### Launch of Graviton5 and Amazon EC2 M9g Instances

The headline announcement of the keynote was the launch of AWS Graviton5-based Amazon EC2 M9g instances. Graviton5 represents the latest generation of AWS's Arm-based processors, engineered to deliver substantial performance improvements for general-purpose workloads while continuing to drive down the energy and cost footprint per unit of compute. The new M9g instances are positioned as the next default choice for customers running scale-out applications, web services, microservices, containerized workloads, and data processing pipelines, building on the broad customer adoption that previous Graviton generations have enjoyed.

## Strategic Implications and Customer Outcomes

### A Culture Built for Long-Term Innovation

Throughout the keynote, DeSantis and Brown returned to the idea that AWS's progress is rooted in a culture that embraces deep technical investment over multi-year horizons. By owning the hardware and software stack end to end, AWS engineers can identify optimization opportunities that span domains — for example, co-designing networking silicon with the hypervisor or tuning processor features to specific service workloads. This integrated approach was presented as the reason AWS can continue to introduce leading-edge solutions at a pace that benefits customers directly through lower prices and higher performance.

### Action Items and Takeaways for Customers

The practical outcome for customers is a clear path forward: workloads currently running on older instance families stand to gain meaningful price-performance improvements by migrating to Graviton5-powered M9g instances. The keynote encouraged architects and engineering leaders to evaluate their general-purpose compute footprint, plan migrations to the newest generation, and take advantage of the broader Graviton ecosystem, which now spans managed services, container platforms, and database offerings. The overarching message was that AWS's silicon-to-services strategy will continue to deliver incremental, customer-visible gains in the years ahead.
