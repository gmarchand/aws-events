# AWS re:Invent 2025 Keynote with Dr. Swami Sivasubramanian

## Overview of the Agentic AI Vision

Dr. Swami Sivasubramanian, Vice President of Agentic AI at AWS, delivered a keynote centered on how agentic AI is fundamentally reshaping the way people live and work. He framed the session around the practical tools, services, and engineering innovations AWS now provides for building, deploying, and operating secure, reliable, and scalable AI agents in production. The keynote balanced near-term, customer-ready announcements with a deeper look at the infrastructure and research advances powering the next generation of agentic systems.

## Major Launches and Announcements

### Expanding the Agent Development Stack with Strands

Swami introduced significant expansions to **Strands Agents**, AWS's open-source framework for building AI agents. Strands now supports **TypeScript**, opening agent development to the vast community of JavaScript and Node.js developers and enabling agentic logic to run natively across web and serverless environments. Strands also gained dedicated support for **robotics**, allowing developers to extend agentic reasoning beyond software into physical systems. This positions Strands as a unifying framework spanning enterprise software agents and embodied agents in the physical world.

### Smarter Agent Memory in Bedrock AgentCore

A central theme was giving agents long-term, human-like memory. AWS announced **episodic memory** for **Amazon Bedrock AgentCore Memory**, enabling agents to retain and recall coherent sequences of past interactions, decisions, and outcomes rather than treating each session as isolated. This allows agents to learn from prior experiences, maintain continuity across conversations, and behave more consistently over time, which is critical for enterprise workflows where context and history determine quality of action.

### Customizing Foundation Models with Reinforcement Fine-Tuning

To help customers shape model behavior for specialized domains, AWS launched **Reinforcement Fine-Tuning (RFT)** in **Amazon Bedrock**. RFT enables organizations to align foundation models with their own goals, evaluation criteria, and reward signals, producing models that reason and respond according to enterprise-specific standards. This was positioned as a step beyond supervised fine-tuning, particularly for tasks requiring nuanced judgment, multi-step reasoning, or domain expertise.

### Deeper Model Customization in SageMaker AI

Complementing Bedrock's managed customization, **SageMaker AI** received expanded **model customization** capabilities. These give advanced practitioners more control over training pipelines, customization techniques, and deployment patterns, supporting customers who need to tailor models at a level of depth that goes beyond out-of-the-box fine-tuning APIs.

### Checkpointless Training on SageMaker HyperPod

For teams training very large models, AWS announced **checkpointless training** on **SageMaker HyperPod**. By rethinking how training state is preserved and recovered, this innovation reduces the operational overhead, storage cost, and time loss traditionally associated with frequent checkpointing during long-running distributed training jobs. The result is faster iteration on frontier models and more efficient use of large GPU clusters.

### Nova Act for Agentic Action-Taking

Closing the major launches, Swami introduced **Nova Act**, an addition to the Nova family aimed squarely at enabling agents to take real actions on behalf of users. Nova Act extends Nova's reasoning capabilities into reliable execution of multi-step tasks across browsers, applications, and tools, advancing AWS's vision of agents that not only converse but also accomplish work end to end.

## Key Themes and Outcomes

### From Models to Production-Ready Agents

The cumulative message of the keynote was that AWS is moving customers from experimenting with foundation models to operating dependable agentic systems in production. Strands provides the framework, AgentCore supplies memory and runtime services, Bedrock and SageMaker handle customization, HyperPod scales the underlying training, and Nova Act delivers action-capable intelligence. Together these announcements form a vertically integrated stack covering every stage of the agent lifecycle.

### Engineering Innovation as a Differentiator

Swami emphasized that agentic AI's promise depends on deep engineering investments — in memory architectures, training efficiency, reinforcement learning techniques, and multi-language framework support. The keynote made clear that AWS intends to compete by lowering the cost, complexity, and risk of running agents at scale, while giving developers flexibility across languages, modalities, and deployment targets, including robotics.

### A Glimpse of What Comes Next

The keynote concluded with a forward-looking outlook in which agents continuously learn from experience through episodic memory, are continually refined via reinforcement signals, and act autonomously across digital and physical environments. Swami positioned this as the foundation for the next era of cloud computing, with AWS providing the secure, reliable, and scalable substrate on which customers will build the agentic applications that transform their industries.
