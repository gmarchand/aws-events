# Seamless Network-based Fraud Protection for FinTech and Digital Business

## The Evolving Fraud Landscape

Online fraud has transformed into an industrialized threat that operates at unprecedented scale and speed. The session, presented by Vonage (part of Ericsson) at AWS re:Invent 2025, frames modern fraud as a relentless ecosystem powered by crime-as-a-service tooling. Attackers now leverage readily available platforms to launch synthetic identity schemes, account takeovers, payment scams, and traffic pumping campaigns, evolving their techniques far faster than legacy defenses can adapt. This shift has fundamentally changed the threat calculus for FinTechs and digital businesses, which face adversaries that are organized, well-funded, and continuously refining their methods.

## The FinTech and Digital Business Dilemma

### Balancing Compliance, Experience, and Risk

FinTechs and digital-first companies operate under intense pressure to satisfy strict regulatory and compliance requirements while simultaneously delivering frictionless customer experiences. The session highlights the inherent tension between these goals: every additional verification step or security check introduces friction that can drive customer churn, abandoned transactions, and lost revenue. At the same time, weakening controls invites direct financial losses and lasting damage to brand trust. The cost of getting this balance wrong is severe, encompassing not only fraud losses themselves but also missed revenue opportunities and erosion of consumer confidence that can take years to rebuild.

### The Limits of Traditional Defenses

Conventional fraud prevention tools — passwords, OTPs, and visible authentication challenges — were designed for a slower threat environment and increasingly fall short against industrialized attacks. They burden legitimate users with friction while remaining vulnerable to social engineering, SIM swaps, and automated attack pipelines. The presentation argues that businesses need a defensive layer that works invisibly in the background, validating users in real time without interrupting their journey.

## Mobile Network Intelligence as an Invisible Defense Layer

### How Network Signals Strengthen Fraud Protection

The core proposition of the talk is that mobile network intelligence provides a powerful, invisible layer of fraud protection. By tapping into real-time signals from mobile operators — such as SIM swap detection, device location, number verification, and account tenure — businesses can validate users and transactions silently, before fraud is committed. These network-derived signals are difficult for attackers to spoof because they originate from the telecom infrastructure itself rather than from the application or device.

### Building Trust While Improving Customer Experience

Integrating these signals through Vonage and Ericsson's network APIs, deployed in conjunction with AWS, allows FinTechs and digital businesses to make smarter risk decisions in milliseconds. Low-risk users proceed seamlessly without additional challenges, while suspicious activity triggers stronger verification only when warranted. The outcome is a dual win: stronger fraud prevention that meets compliance obligations, paired with smoother onboarding, faster checkouts, and higher conversion rates. By making security invisible to legitimate customers and unforgiving to fraudsters, organizations can simultaneously protect revenue, reinforce brand trust, and stay ahead of an increasingly industrialized threat landscape.
