# Intelligent Security: Protection at Scale from Development to Production

## Adapting Security Practices for the AI Era

In this AWS re:Invent 2025 session, AWS Vice President and Chief Information Security Officer Amy Herzog addresses a fundamental challenge facing modern security organizations: the rapid acceleration of software creation enabled by AI tooling, paired with the parallel acceleration of threat actor capabilities using those same technologies. As builders generate more solutions faster than ever before, security teams must rethink how they scale protections, evaluate risk, and embed safeguards across the entire software lifecycle. Herzog frames this shift as both an opportunity and an imperative, arguing that traditional manual security practices cannot keep pace with the volume and velocity of AI-assisted development.

## Architectural Choices and Technical Tradeoffs

Herzog walks through the architectural decisions security leaders must confront when integrating AI-powered protections into their environments. She emphasizes that adopting intelligent security is not a single product purchase but a series of deliberate tradeoffs involving where to apply automation, how to balance preventative versus detective controls, and how to maintain human judgment for the decisions that matter most. Drawing on AWS's own experience operating at massive scale, she illustrates how layered defenses, automated reasoning, and continuous validation work together to reduce the burden on security engineers while raising the overall security posture. The discussion highlights how organizations can avoid common pitfalls, such as over-reliance on AI without proper guardrails or under-investment in the foundational telemetry needed to make automated decisions trustworthy.

### Real-World Strategies for AI-Powered Protection

Using concrete examples from customer environments and AWS internal practices, Herzog shares actionable strategies for implementing automated protections from development through production. She describes how shifting security left into the developer experience, combined with continuous runtime monitoring informed by AI, allows teams to identify and remediate issues earlier and at lower cost. The session underscores the importance of meeting builders where they work, embedding security guidance directly into the tools and AI assistants they already use, so that secure choices become the path of least resistance. Herzog closes by encouraging security leaders to invest in the data foundations, automation pipelines, and cultural changes required to operate security as a scalable, intelligent system rather than a bottleneck, positioning their organizations to defend effectively against adversaries who are themselves rapidly adopting AI.
