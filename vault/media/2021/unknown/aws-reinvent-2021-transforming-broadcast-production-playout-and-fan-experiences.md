# Transforming Broadcast Production, Playout, and Fan Experiences on AWS

## Overview of the Session

This AWS re:Invent 2021 session brings together two major broadcasters, Discovery and ViacomCBS, to share how they are transforming their broadcast operations using AWS cloud services. The discussion centers on modernizing legacy broadcast infrastructure, enabling global channel playout, enriching fan experiences through data science, and evolving live sports production workflows. The overarching theme is the migration of traditionally on-premises, hardware-bound broadcast operations into agile, cost-effective, cloud-native environments that deliver measurable business outcomes.

## Discovery's Broadcast Transformation Journey

### Modernizing the Supply Chain and Global Playout

Discovery describes its multi-year effort to reimagine the entire broadcast supply chain on AWS, an initiative recognized with an Emmy Award for technical achievement. By moving content ingest, processing, and distribution workflows to the cloud, the company replaced rigid, capital-intensive infrastructure with elastic services that scale on demand. This transformation extends to the playout of linear channels worldwide, allowing Discovery to launch and operate channels across multiple regions without deploying traditional broadcast facilities in each location. The most striking outcome is a 61% reduction in infrastructure costs, achieved by eliminating duplicated hardware, consolidating operations, and leveraging pay-as-you-go cloud economics. The modernized supply chain also accelerates content time-to-market and improves operational resilience.

### Enhancing Eurosport Audience Experiences with Data Science

Discovery's Eurosport brand uses AWS data science and analytics services to deliver next-generation audience experiences. By applying machine learning to sports content, viewer behavior, and contextual signals, Eurosport personalizes recommendations, surfaces richer storytelling around athletes and events, and engages fans across digital touchpoints. These capabilities turn live sports broadcasts into interactive, data-driven experiences that deepen viewer loyalty and open new avenues for engagement beyond traditional linear viewing.

## ViacomCBS and the Evolution of Live Sports Production

### Maturing Live Production Workloads in the Cloud

ViacomCBS shares its ongoing journey of moving live sports production into AWS, an area historically considered the most challenging to migrate due to ultra-low-latency, high-reliability requirements. The company describes how it progressed from initial experiments toward production-grade live workflows, partnering with major independent software vendors to bring established broadcast tools into the cloud environment. This combination of AWS infrastructure and ISV solutions allows ViacomCBS to orchestrate complex live productions with the flexibility of remote, distributed teams.

### Challenges, Solutions, and Outcomes

The discussion candidly addresses the obstacles encountered along the way, including managing latency for live signals, integrating with existing on-premises systems, ensuring redundancy for mission-critical broadcasts, and adapting operational practices for cloud-based workflows. The solutions involve a mix of architectural patterns, dedicated network connectivity, and close collaboration with technology partners to harden cloud-based live production for the demands of professional sports. The result is a more agile production model that supports faster innovation, broader collaboration, and the ability to deliver live sports content at scale while progressively reducing reliance on traditional broadcast facilities.
