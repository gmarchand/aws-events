# Finishing Workflows in the Cloud with Autodesk Flame

## Bringing Industry-Leading Post-Production to AWS

Autodesk Flame, long regarded as a benchmark tool for visual effects, finishing, compositing, and color grading in film and television production, is now available on Amazon Web Services. This marks a significant milestone for the post-production industry, as professionals can for the first time access the full suite of Flame's industry-leading capabilities through cloud infrastructure rather than relying exclusively on specialized on-premises workstations. The transition opens new possibilities for studios, freelancers, and production houses that have traditionally been constrained by the cost, location, and maintenance demands of high-end finishing hardware.

## Performance That Meets or Exceeds On-Premises Hardware

A central concern for any creative professional considering a move to the cloud is whether performance will hold up under the demanding workloads typical of visual effects and finishing work. Running Flame on AWS delivers performance levels that meet or exceed those of dedicated on-premises systems, ensuring that artists can work with the responsiveness and reliability they expect from professional finishing tools. This parity in performance means that complex compositing tasks, color grading sessions, and finishing workflows can be executed in the cloud without compromise, removing what has historically been one of the largest barriers to cloud adoption in high-end content creation.

### Expanding Access for VFX, Film, TV, and Compositing Professionals

By making Flame accessible through AWS, the offering broadens the reach of professional post-production tools to a wider community of visual effects artists, film and television editors, compositors, and finishing specialists. Cloud availability supports flexible, distributed workflows where teams can collaborate across geographies, scale resources to match project demands, and reduce the capital investment required to enter or expand within the industry. The result is a more agile production environment in which creative teams can focus on their craft while leveraging the elasticity and global infrastructure of AWS to support their finishing pipelines.
