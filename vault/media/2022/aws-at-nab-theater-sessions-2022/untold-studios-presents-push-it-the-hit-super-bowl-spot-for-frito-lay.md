# Behind the Scenes of "Push It": Untold Studios' Super Bowl Spot for Frito-Lay

## Bringing a Flamin' Hot Vision to Life

Untold Studios partnered with Frito-Lay to produce "Push It," a high-profile Super Bowl commercial featuring a chorus of singing and break-dancing CGI creatures. The spot demanded a level of creative ambition rarely attempted within the compressed timelines that define major broadcast advertising, requiring the studio to deliver seven fully realized digital characters performing complex choreography and lip-synced vocals. The result is a visually striking piece of work designed to capture the attention of one of the largest television audiences of the year, leveraging humor, music, and character animation to promote the Frito-Lay brand during the Super Bowl.

## A Globally Distributed Production Powered by the Cloud

The production assembled 45 artists from around the world to collaborate on the project, an arrangement made possible by Untold Studios' cloud-based studio infrastructure built on AWS. Rather than relying on a single physical location, the team operated as a unified creative unit across geographies, sharing assets, render capacity, and review sessions through their AWS environment. This cloud-native approach allowed Untold to scale resources dynamically to meet the demands of high-end CGI work, tapping specialist talent wherever it resided and keeping the pipeline moving around the clock.

### Delivering Seven CGI Creatures in Record Time

The technical centerpiece of the project was the creation of seven CGI creatures, each requiring detailed design, rigging, animation, lighting, and rendering to perform convincingly as singers and break-dancers. By running their entire studio in the cloud on AWS, Untold compressed what would traditionally be a lengthy production schedule into a record-fast turnaround, ensuring the spot was ready for its Super Bowl debut. The collaboration demonstrates how cloud-based production pipelines enable creative studios to take on ambitious, time-critical projects at scale while maintaining the visual quality expected of premier broadcast advertising.
