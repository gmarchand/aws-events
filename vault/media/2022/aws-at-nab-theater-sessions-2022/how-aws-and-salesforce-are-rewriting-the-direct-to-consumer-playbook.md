# Rewriting the Direct-to-Consumer Streaming Playbook with AWS and Salesforce

## The Challenge of Building Profitable D2C Streaming Services

Building and scaling a profitable direct-to-consumer (D2C) streaming service has become one of the most demanding undertakings for modern content owners and providers. While the market opportunity for streaming continues to expand, the operational complexity behind a successful service has grown in parallel. Content providers must simultaneously identify potential subscribers, engage them with compelling experiences, and retain them through carefully timed monetization offers. Each of these stages requires a sophisticated strategy supported by an equally sophisticated technology stack, and the integration burden across customer data, marketing, billing, and video delivery systems has historically slowed innovation and inflated costs.

The session frames this difficulty as the central problem the industry needs to solve. Without a unified approach to subscriber lifecycle management and content delivery, providers struggle to deliver the personalized, frictionless experiences that today's viewers expect. The result is often higher churn, weaker monetization, and missed opportunities to convert casual viewers into loyal, paying subscribers.

## A Joint Reference Implementation from AWS and Salesforce

To address these pain points, AWS and Salesforce collaborated on a customizable reference implementation that fuses Salesforce's subscriber lifecycle capabilities with AWS's content viewing and streaming infrastructure. The integration is designed to give content providers a single, coherent foundation rather than forcing them to stitch together disparate systems on their own. Salesforce contributes its strengths in identifying, engaging, and retaining customers, including managing subscriptions, personalizing offers, and orchestrating marketing journeys. AWS contributes the scalable media services required to ingest, process, secure, and deliver high-quality video to global audiences.

The joint solution emphasizes three architectural priorities: security, scalability, and ease of deployment. By pre-integrating the most challenging connection points between the two platforms, the reference implementation removes much of the undifferentiated heavy lifting that typically consumes engineering resources during a streaming service launch or expansion. Providers can adopt the blueprint as-is or customize it to fit their specific business models, whether subscription-based, ad-supported, transactional, or a hybrid approach.

### Outcomes for Content Providers

The practical outcome of this collaboration is a faster, more predictable path to launching next-generation streaming and monetization experiences. Content providers gain the ability to align viewing behavior with subscriber data in real time, which enables more accurate targeting, smarter retention strategies, and timely monetization offers tailored to each viewer's stage in the lifecycle. Because the reference implementation is designed to be deployed with minimal friction, teams can shift their focus from infrastructure plumbing to differentiating their product, content catalog, and audience relationships. Ultimately, the AWS and Salesforce partnership offers a modernized playbook for D2C streaming, one that helps providers move beyond fragmented tooling and build services that are both economically sustainable and engaging for viewers.
