# AWS Color in the Cloud: Grading and Finishing with Baselight

## Overview of the Cloud-Based Color Workflow

AWS, in collaboration with FilmLight, has introduced a high-fidelity color-grading workflow that operates entirely in the cloud, addressing one of the most demanding use cases in post-production. Color grading and finishing traditionally require precise reference monitoring, low-latency interaction, and uncompromised image fidelity—requirements that have historically tied colorists to on-premises facilities with specialized hardware. By combining the AWS Elemental suite of broadcast products with the visually lossless JPEG XS codec, AWS now delivers a workflow capable of supporting up to 12-bit 4:4:4 reference monitoring, meeting the exacting standards of professional color grading and finishing applications.

This integration with FilmLight's Baselight grading system allows artists to operate a familiar, industry-standard tool from a remote location while preserving the visual quality and responsiveness expected in a high-end grading suite. The workflow is designed not only to serve the colorist at the primary station but also to extend collaboration to directors and directors of photography through over-the-shoulder view integration, enabling creative review sessions that mirror the experience of being physically present in the grading bay.

## Technical Architecture and Performance

### High-Fidelity Reference Monitoring with JPEG XS

At the core of the solution is the JPEG XS codec, a visually lossless compression standard that preserves the color accuracy and detail required for professional reference monitoring. AWS leverages this codec alongside its Elemental broadcast services to transmit imagery at up to 12-bit 4:4:4 quality, ensuring that the colors a remote artist sees on a calibrated reference display match what would be produced in a traditional facility. This level of fidelity is essential for delivering broadcast and cinematic content where subtle tonal decisions affect the final viewer experience.

### Offloading Workloads to Managed Services

A defining characteristic of the AWS approach is the offloading of heavy processing tasks to managed services rather than running them on the same instance that hosts the Baselight application. This architectural decision ensures that the artist's interactive experience remains responsive and that the host instance is dedicated to the grading application itself. By separating the encoding, transmission, and monitoring pipelines from the application workload, AWS avoids the performance compromises that often accompany cloud-based creative tools, allowing colorists to work without the latency or sluggishness that would otherwise undermine creative output.

### Collaborative Review Capabilities

The workflow's support for over-the-shoulder views extends the cloud grading session beyond the colorist, giving directors and DPs the ability to participate in review sessions remotely while still seeing accurate representations of the work in progress. This capability preserves the collaborative nature of color grading, where creative decisions are typically made jointly between the colorist and other stakeholders, and adapts it to a distributed production environment.

## Outcomes and Significance for Post-Production

The partnership between AWS and FilmLight demonstrates that even the most fidelity-sensitive post-production tasks can be moved to the cloud without sacrificing artistic quality or workflow integrity. Productions gain flexibility in where and how they staff color grading work, can scale resources on demand, and can bring remote collaborators into sessions seamlessly. By pairing Baselight's established grading capabilities with AWS Elemental services and JPEG XS transport, the solution delivers a professional-grade color pipeline suited to broadcast, episodic, and feature finishing—signaling a meaningful shift in how high-end color workflows can be deployed across the industry.
