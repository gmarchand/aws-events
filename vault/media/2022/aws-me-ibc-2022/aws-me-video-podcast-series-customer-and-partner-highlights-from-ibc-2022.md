# AWS M&E Video Podcast: Customer and Partner Highlights from IBC 2022

## Returning to In-Person Industry Engagement

The AWS Media & Entertainment Video Podcast dedicated a special episode to IBC 2022, capturing the energy and significance of the broadcasting industry's return to in-person gathering after a prolonged pause caused by global circumstances. The episode brings together a diverse group of AWS customers and partners who attended the show in Amsterdam, providing a multifaceted perspective on what the event meant for the media and entertainment community. Participants reflected on the value of face-to-face conversations, spontaneous collaboration, and the renewed sense of momentum that comes with reconnecting with peers, clients, and technology providers in a physical setting.

## Industry Themes and Cloud-Driven Transformation

A recurring thread throughout the discussions centered on the accelerating shift toward cloud-based workflows across the broadcast and media production landscape. Customers and partners shared how their organizations are leveraging AWS services to modernize content creation, distribution, and monetization pipelines, with particular emphasis on live production, remote collaboration, streaming infrastructure, and machine learning–enhanced media workflows. Conversations highlighted how the industry has matured in its adoption of cloud technologies, moving from experimentation to production-grade deployments that support mission-critical operations. The dialogue also addressed the evolving expectations of audiences, the pressures on operational efficiency, and the ways in which scalable cloud architectures are enabling broadcasters and content owners to respond with greater agility.

### Customer and Partner Perspectives on Show Highlights

Individual guests spoke to the specific announcements, demonstrations, and partnerships that resonated most strongly with them at IBC 2022. Several emphasized the depth of innovation showcased on the AWS stand and across partner exhibits, pointing to advancements in areas such as cloud-native live production, content supply chain automation, direct-to-consumer streaming platforms, and immersive viewing experiences. Partners discussed how their integrations with AWS services are helping joint customers reduce time to market, optimize costs, and unlock new creative possibilities. Customers, in turn, described practical outcomes from their cloud journeys, sharing lessons learned and outlining their priorities for the months ahead. Collectively, the highlights painted a picture of an industry that is energized, collaborative, and increasingly confident in the role of cloud as the foundation for the next generation of media experiences.
